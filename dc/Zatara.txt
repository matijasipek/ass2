Like the very similar [[Mandrake the Magician]], Zatara had a large East Indian as a friend/bodyguard, called Tong, to share his early adventures.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Markstein |first1=Don |title=Zatara the Magician |url=http://www.toonopedia.com/zatara.htm |website=Don Markstein\'s Toonopedia |access-date=23 April 2020}}</ref>\n\nAs well as being an [[magic (illusion)|illusionist]], Zatara also had genuine magical powers (decades later ascribed to being a descendant of the \'\'[[Homo magi]]\'\'), which he focused through speaking backwards: he could do anything so long as he could describe it in {{not a typo|\'\'sdrawkcab hceeps\'\'}} ("backwards speech").<ref>{{cite book |last1=Mitchell |first1=Kurt |last2=Thomas |first2=Roy |title=American Comic Book Chronicles: 1940-1944 |date=2019 |publisher=TwoMorrows Publishing |isbn=978-1605490892 |page=25}}</ref> This helped distinguish Zatara from the numerous [[Mandrake the Magician]] knockoffs that cluttered the [[comic]]s and [[pulp magazine]]s of the day, although [[Merlin the Magician (comics)|Merlin the Magician]] ([[Quality Comics]]) also had this attribute, and it was also given to him by Zatara\'s creator, [[Fred Guardineer]].\n\nAccording to \'\'Jess Nevins\' Encyclopedia of Golden Age Superheroes\'\', "he fights Egyptian wizards, an evil Druid, Zulus, a Mad Lama, Mongol warriors, the Gorilla King, a Saturnian terror, and Moon Men".<ref>{{cite book |last1=Nevins |first1=Jess |title=Encyclopedia of Golden Age Superheroes |date=2013 |publisher=High Rock Press |isbn=978-1-61318-023-5 |page=303}}</ref>\n\nHis love of magic began early when he was given a [[magic kit]] by his uncle, himself a professional illusionist. Although he began learning the craft in childhood, his early attempts at performing professionally were unsuccessful until he realized that he needed to work on his [[Showmanship (performing)|showmanship]].\n\nHis lessons in magic were bolstered by visits from the [[Phantom Stranger]], and he had a sexual relationship with [[Madame Xanadu]], but she refused to marry him because she saw his future.<ref>\'\'Madame Xanadu Disenchanted\'\' #9 (2009)</ref>\n\nTo that end, he acquired the old diaries of [[Leonardo da Vinci]], who was a direct ancestor. While reading the diaries, which Da Vinci wrote in backwards spelling as a security precaution, Zatara learned that his family had the command of magic. He discovered this inadvertently when he accidentally gave a command to a [[mannequin]] to begin waving an arm wildly. Zatara realized that he could command it to stop by giving the order in backwards spelling.\n\nWith this new knowledge, Zatara developed a successful show. During the premiere performance, a fire broke out on stage, forcing Zatara to use his command of real magic to put it out. While the audience mistook the incident as part of the act, Zatara realized that this power could be invaluable in helping people and he resolved to use it as such between shows.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Conway|first1=Gerry|last2=Tanghal|first2=Romeo|title=The Secret Spell!|journal=DC Special Blue Ribbon Digest|date=December 1980|volume=|issue=#5}}</ref>\n\nZatara became good friends with [[Thomas Wayne]]. His excursions with Wayne led to Wayne meeting his wife, [[Martha Wayne|Martha]]. After the two were killed, Zatara left Gotham City, blaming himself for being unable to stop the orphaning of young Bruce. Eventually, in Europe, Zatara would meet and wed Sindella, who gave birth to their daughter, [[Zatanna]]. Sindella seemingly died after giving birth to Zatanna, prompting Zatara to become a depressed drunk.\n\nThings changed for the better in Zatara\'s life when a young Bruce Wayne arrived, requesting Zatara to teach him to become an escape artist and illusionist. Bruce\'s appearance prompted Zatara to address his alcoholism.\n\n===Zatanna\'s search===\nZatanna, Zatara\'s daughter by Sindella, a fellow \'\'[[Homo magi]]\'\',<ref>\'\'The Spectre\'\' (vol. 2) #7</ref> was introduced in a multipart [[Fictional crossover|crossover]] in which she attempted to find her father, and he attempted to stop her at every turn, because he knew that a spell had been placed upon them both that would cause them to both die if they saw one another. This adventure includes Zatara confronting an evil warlock on a world where nothing changes or grows older. Zatara steals his adversary\'s teleportation crystal in a somewhat-successful attempt to stop the man from invading Earth.<ref>\'\'Green Lantern\'\' (vol. 2) #42 (January 1966)</ref> Zatara ends up trapped in the land of Kharma by a sorceress called Allura, whom he had trapped in the Sword of [[Paracelsus]]. Allura turned out to have a [[Evil twin|good twin]], also called Allura, who forced her to remove the spell.<ref>\'\'[[Justice League of America]]\'\' #51</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Cowsill |first1=Alan |last2=Irvine |first2=Alex |last3=Manning |first3=Matthew K. |last4=McAvennie |first4=Michael |last5=Wallace |first5=Daniel |title=DC Comics Year By Year: A Visual Chronicle |date=2019 |publisher=DK Publishing |isbn=978-1-4654-8578-6 |page=174}}</ref>\n\n===Death===\nAt the conclusion of [[Alan Moore]]\'s "American Gothic" storyline<ref>Chronicled in \'\'[[Swamp Thing]]\'\' (vol. 2) #35-50 (April 1985-July 1986).</ref> (which was tied to the events of \'\'[[Crisis on Infinite Earths]]\'\'), [[John Constantine]] comes to get [[Zatanna]], [[Mento (comics)|Mento]] and [[Sargon the Sorcerer]] to come together to help demonic and divine forces in other hellish dimensions battle the entity known as the \'Great Evil Beast\'. The séance is held at Wintersgate Manor, the home of [[Baron Winters]] in Georgetown, Washington D.C., which is also a temporal threshold to other planes of reality. Because Constantine had previously taken Zatanna to a "[[Neotantra|tantric]] studies meeting", Zatara will \'\'not\'\' let Zatanna out of his sight with Constantine present and, by his very presence, is forced to take part in the [[seance]] to which he was \'\'not\'\' invited. The Beast, which is so tall that its thumb alone looms over Hell, takes notice of their group twice. The first glimpse dooms Sargon, whom Zatara convinces to \'die like a sorcerer\' and not break the holding of hands. Sargon burns to death nobly. The second glimpse starts to literally heat up Zatanna. Zatara willingly takes the effect onto himself, dying (his smoldering hat lands on the table), but sparing his daughter\'s life.<ref>\'\'Swamp Thing\'\' (vol. 2) #49-50 (June-July 1986)</ref>\n\n===Afterlife problems===\nSince then he has made sporadic appearances in the afterlife, including resurrecting Mason O\'Dare in \'\'[[Starman (comics)|Starman]]\'\' (vol. 2) #80, and the \'\'Seven Soldiers: Zatanna\'\' miniseries.<ref>\'\'Seven Soldiers: Zatanna\'\' #1–4 (2005)</ref> In the \'\'[[Reign in Hell]]\'\' miniseries, Zatara is part of a general resistance movement operating in Hell. He is caught up in the various conflicts and is slain a second time in Hell by a rampaging [[Lobo (DC Comics)|Lobo]]. As with most of the \'dead\', he risks becoming fodder for Hell, a torment where the physicality of the damned is used for general resources, such as building material. By manipulating his blood to form words, he asks Zatanna to consign his soul to the \'Abyss\', a realm that Hell cannot touch. Zatanna does so, tormented that she must now destroy her father\'s essence.<ref>\'\'Reign in Hell\'\' #5</ref>\n\nHis nephew [[Zachary Zatara|Zachary]] now uses the Zatara name as a stage magician, going so far, in the alternate future of the \'\'[[Titans Tomorrow]]\'\' timeline, to model his physical appearance and heroic getup on the vintage clothing and grooming of his late uncle.\n\nIn \'\'Justice League of America\'\' (vol. 2) #39–40, a tie-in to the \'\'[[Blackest Night]]\'\' crossover event, Zatara was reanimated as a member of the [[Black Lantern Corps]], ready to attack his daughter, Zatanna, in the [[Hall of Justice (comics)|Hall of Justice]]. Zatanna is successful in banishing the Black Lantern, but was left psychologically crushed from having to again kill her father (after watching him die once before).<ref>\'\'Justice League of America\'\' (vol. 2) #39–40</ref>\n\n==Other versions==\n===\'\'Kingdom Come\'\'===\nAnother Zatara was featured in a supporting role in the miniseries \'\'[[Kingdom Come (comic)|Kingdom Come]]\'\' and its follow-up, \'\'[[The Kingdom (comic)|The Kingdom]]\'\'.
