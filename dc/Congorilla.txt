Originating in \'\'More Fun Comics\'\' #56, the strip was a moderate success and ran there until issue #67 (May 1941), after which it moved to \'\'[[Action Comics]]\'\' from issue #37 (June 1941).\n\n\'\'Action Comics\'\' #191 (April 1954) saw the introduction of [[Janu the Jungle Boy]], a young boy brought up in the jungle after his father had been killed by a tiger. The character finally received a [[Limited series (comics)|mini-series]] of his own in [[1992 in comics|1992]], where Congo Bill is betrayed by his (now corrupt) ward Janu, who usurps the Congorilla identity and Bill is forced to fight his adopted son to the death. Congo Bill became blind in his left eye, making it hard for him to see after fighting his son to the end. In [[1999 in comics|1999]], DC Comics once again brought Congo Bill back for another four-issue [[Limited series (comics)|limited series]] under the company\'s mature readership Vertigo Comics imprint.\n\nCongorilla returned in the 2009 series \'\'[[Cry for Justice]]\'\', joining a proactive splinter faction of the [[Justice League]]. At one point he was a member of the [[Irish Republican Army|IRA]],<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=25567 | title=WC10: Spotlight on James Robinson | last=Evans | first=Chris | work=[[Comic Book Resources]] | date=April 5, 2010 | access-date=June 17, 2010}}</ref> and during [[World War I]] he served as soldier in the [[Battle of the Somme]] in France 1916 and also Battle of Flanders Field in Passendale, Belgium. He worked his way up a spy in [[Austria]].<ref>\'\'Justice League of America\'\' (vol. 2) #44 (June 2010)</ref> He later became a globe-trotting adventurer, and for a time worked for the Worldwide Insurance Company, protecting policies they had written and saving the company from fraudulent payouts.\n\nBill grew content to live in his adopted [[Africa]]n home, swearing to protect it from harm. There he befriended a witch-doctor known as Chief Kawolo. When Kawolo was mortally injured in a fall, he summoned Bill to his bedside, and offered him a magic ring. Kawolo told the skeptical Congo Bill that, by rubbing the ring, he could transfer his consciousness into the body of the legendary Golden Gorilla. He accepted the ring to humor his friend\'s dying wish. Several weeks later, an earthquake trapped Bill in a deep cave. With no possible escape, Congo Bill hopelessly rubbed the magic ring. Instantly, his mind was transported into the body of the Golden Gorilla.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Markstein |first1=Don |title=Congorilla |url=http://www.toonopedia.com/congoril.htm |website=Don Markstein\'s Toonopedia |access-date=2 April 2020 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120917144634/http://www.toonopedia.com/congoril.htm |archive-date=17 September 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Racing to the cave-in, he used his massive strength to clear the blocked entrance and wondering what had become of his body without him being "home". He realized that when his consciousness entered the body of the Golden Gorilla, the creature\'s consciousness entered his own body. Bill decides to use his new powers to fight crime in the jungle. He is later assisted by Janu, a young boy raised in the jungle.<ref name="dc-ency">{{Cite book | last=Greenberger | first=Robert | author-link=Robert Greenberger | contribution=Congorilla | editor-last=Dougall | editor-first=Alastair | title=The DC Comics Encyclopedia | page=87 | publisher=[[Dorling Kindersley]] | location=[[New York City|New York]] | year=2008 | isbn=978-0-7566-4119-1 | oclc=213309017}}</ref>\n\n===Justice League===\nYears later, Bill (now known as Congorilla) is trapped in his gorilla form upon the death of his human body. He becomes the protector of a band of gorillas, and friend of the [[South Africa]]n hero [[Freedom Beast]]. When the gorillas and Freedom Beast are slaughtered by hunters, Congorilla decides to seek justice.<ref>\'\'Justice League: Cry for Justice\'\' #1</ref> After the trail leads to the villain [[Prometheus (DC Comics)|Prometheus]], Congorilla teams up with [[Mikaal Tomas|Starman]], and eventually a splinter faction of the Justice League. After Prometheus\'s death, Congorilla becomes a full-time member of the Justice League.\n\nDuring his time with the League, Bill faces off against such foes as the rogue [[Alan Scott|Starheart]]<ref>\'\'Justice Society of America\'\' (vol. 3) #41 (September 2010)</ref> and [[Eclipso]], and becomes close friends with Starman and [[Supergirl (Kara Zor-El)|Supergirl]]. He is also part of a much larger makeshift Justice League squad when the return of Batman from a seeming death threatens to destroy time and space.<ref>\'\'Batman: The Return of Bruce Wayne\'\' #1-6 (May–November 2010)</ref> After [[Batman Inc.]] appoints the vigilante [[Batwing (DC Comics)|Batwing]] as the official Batman of Africa, Congorilla realizes that the continent is too big for one hero to handle. He ultimately chooses to resign from the JLA in order to help organize the superheroes of Africa into a more efficient team, as well as to find a worthy successor to carry on Freedom Beast\'s legacy.<ref>\'\'Justice League\'\' (vol. 2) #60</ref>\n\n==Powers==\nBefore becoming Congorilla, Bill was a skilled hunter and aviator.
