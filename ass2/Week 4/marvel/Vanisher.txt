He commits a series of spectacular crimes including: building a large criminal organization, stealing confidential defense plans and attempting to extort millions of dollars from the U.S. government.<ref>{{cite book |last =Rovin |first =Jeff |title =The Encyclopedia of Supervillains |publisher =Facts on File |date =1987 |location =New York |isbn = 0-8160-1356-X |page=361}}</ref> With support from a large group of non-mutant followers, he goes to the front lawn of the [[White House]] to await his payment. [[Professor X]]avier and the [[X-Men]] are able to intervene when Xavier induces [[amnesia]] upon the Vanisher. Forgetting his powers, the Vanisher is taken into police custody and the X-Men defeat the Vanisher\'s mob.<ref>\'\'X-Men\'\' vol. 1 #2</ref>\n\nThe Vanisher regains his memory and joins [[Factor Three]], a subversive organization of mutants who seek world domination. Factor Three attempts to start [[World War&nbsp;III]] between the United States and the [[Soviet Union]]. The [[Mutant Master]], leader of Factor Three, is revealed to be an alien of the [[List of Marvel Comics alien races#S|Siris]] race with a plan to conquer Earth. The rest of Factor Three, including the Vanisher, team up with the X-Men to defeat him.<ref>\'\'X-Men\'\' vol. 1 #37-39</ref> The Vanisher is later held prisoner by the [[Sentinel (comics)|Sentinels]], but is saved by the X-Men.<ref>\'\'X-Men\'\' vol. 1 #60</ref>\n\nThe Vanisher disappears for a period, but comes out of hiding to fight the [[Champions (1975 team)|Champions]] by joining the [[Brotherhood of Mutants]] and using reprogrammed Sentinels. He is defeated by the Champions and is trapped in mid-teleportation by [[Darkstar (Marvel Comics)|Darkstar]].<ref>\'\'Champions\'\' #17</ref> The [[Darkforce]] strands half of him in another dimension, with the other half in [[Poughkeepsie, New York]] where he is found by the X-Men. When [[Nightcrawler (comics)|Nightcrawler]] touches him they become stranded in another dimension. After returning to Earth,<ref>\'\'Bizarre Adventures\'\' #27</ref> the Vanisher organizes a group of young mutant thieves, known as the [[Fallen Angels (comics)|Fallen Angels]]. Eventually the Fallen Angels disband, and the Vanisher is left to his own devices.<ref>\'\'Fallen Angels\'\' #1-8</ref> Later, he joins a group of villains to challenge the Fantastic Four but is defeated.<ref>\'\'Fantastic Four\'\' vol. 1 #335</ref>\n\nThe Vanisher withdraws again until he is mentally controlled by [[Asylum (comics)|Asylum]] to attack the [[New Warriors]] and other superheroes. Darkling, a [[superhuman]] who uses energies from the [[Darkforce]] Dimension, is defeated and the Vanisher regains his free will.<ref>\'\'New Warriors\'\' #33–34; \'\'New Warriors Annual\'\' #3</ref> He later joins a second incarnation of the [[Enforcers (comics)|Enforcers]], called the [[New Enforcers]]. Along with [[Eel (comics)|Eel]] and a woman named Blitz, the Vanisher is part of the Outer Circle of the Enforcers that becomes involved in a clash between the criminal organizations [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]] and [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]]. The New Enforcers are confronted and eventually defeated by [[Spider-Man]].<ref>\'\'[[Web of Spider-Man]]\'\' #99–100</ref>\n\n===X-Teams===\nThe Vanisher seeks to understand his powers in greater detail. [[Cyclops (Marvel Comics)|Cyclops]] ordered [[X-Force]] to recover it at all costs. Apparently, the Vanisher traverses the [[Darkforce]] Dimension when teleporting from one place on Earth to another. His power appears to be [[psionic]] in nature. The limits on the Vanisher\'s teleportation range and the maximum amount of mass he can teleport with himself are unknown, but he was able to teleport himself and several others from southern California to New York without any visible strain. The Vanisher has a subconscious [[extra-sensory perception|extrasensory]] ability that prevents him from materializing part or all of his body within a solid object, even if he has never before been to the area he is teleporting to. This allows the Vanisher to teleport with greater ease than Nightcrawler. His powers once interacted with those of Nightcrawler, sending them both into alternate dimensions.<ref>\'\'[[Marvel Preview#Bizarre Adventures|Bizarre Adventures]]\'\' Vol 1 #27</ref>\n\nThe Vanisher has used guns which shoot gas or energy beams.
