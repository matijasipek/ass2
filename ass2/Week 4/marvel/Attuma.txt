For unknown reasons Attuma was born with strength, endurance, and speed far superior to those of any normal Atlantean. Attuma quickly became leader of his tribe and declared that he would conquer the city of Atlantis as an ancient prophecy predicted. Attuma had an illegitimate daughter, [[Andromeda (Marvel Comics)|Andromeda]] by a woman named Lady Gelva, but he did not know she gave birth to his only child until confronted by Andromeda.<ref name="Iron Man Annual #10">\'\'Iron Man\'\' Annual #10</ref> Attuma was disappointed to learn he had a child who wasn\'t a boy and was disgusted to find out Lady Gelva was the mother as he saw her as a "bullocked whale". Andromeda would later challenge her father as is the right of every first born male child, Attuma allowed the challenge to proceed because Andromeda had been raised as a warrior. Attuma won the challenge but Andromeda was kidnapped by Ghaur before Attuma could kill her.<ref name="X-Factor Annual #4">\'\'X-Factor\'\' Annual #4</ref>\n\nAttuma gathered his troops and attacked Atlantis, and was defeated by [[Namor|Namor the Sub-Mariner]], the Prince of Atlantis and his allies the [[Fantastic Four]].<ref>\'\'Fantastic Four\'\' #33</ref> Attuma and his troops were banished from Atlantis once again. Over the years, Attuma has been at war with both Atlantis and the "surface world" (the Atlantean name for the human nations). He developed a super-weapon with which to invade the surface world, but was foiled by [[Iron Man]].<ref>\'\'Tales to Astonish\'\' #66</ref> Attuma was among the villains assembled by [[Doctor Doom]] using a mind-control device in one of his attempts to destroy the Fantastic Four. As his troops left the docks they were knocked back in by a bomb in a [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]] truck [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]] had caused to go towards the docks. Mister Fantastic causes the Atlanteans and the other villains to forget this event.<ref>\'\'Fantastic Four\'\' Annual #3</ref> He created tidal waves in another attempted invasion of the surface world, and was this time defeated by the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], after they were alerted by the kidnap of the [[Wasp (comics)|Wasp]], although Attuma nearly succeeded in drowning them.<ref>\'\'The Avengers\'\' #26-27</ref> Attuma enslaved the nomadic Atlanteans after the destruction of Atlantis.<ref>\'\'Sub-Mariner\'\' #4</ref> After Atlantis was rebuilt, Attuma attempted another invasion during the wedding of Namor and [[Lady Dorma|Dorma]].<ref>\'\'Sub-Mariner\'\' #36-37</ref> Attuma allied with the [[Red Ghost (character)|Red Ghost]], and used mind-controlled sea creatures in another attempted conquest of the surface world, and was this time defeated by the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]].<ref>\'\'The Defenders\'\' #7-8</ref> With [[Lemuel Dorcas|Dr. Dorcas]] and [[Tiger Shark (Marvel Comics)|Tiger Shark]], Attuma invaded [[Hydro-Base]], but was then defeated by a teaming of Namor and Doctor Doom.<ref>\'\'Super-Villain Team-Up\'\' #1-3</ref> Attuma then enslaved the Avengers to use them against Namor, but unintentionally instigated a battle between the Avengers and Doctor Doom. Attuma was defeated by Namor, Doom, and the Avengers.<ref>\'\'The Avengers\'\' #154-156; \'\'Super-Villain Team-Up\'\' #9</ref>\n\nWhen the ruling Council of Atlantis asked Prince Namor to abdicate the throne, Namor obliged and left Atlantis. Attuma was hired with his men as mercenaries to end the Atlantean civil war. Several Atlantean nobles battled for the throne, but Attuma took the throne and became the new ruler of Atlantis. His rule was not a popular one though and Attuma kidnapped [[Marrina Smallwood]], Namor\'s new wife to embarrass Namor in the eyes of the Atlantean people.<ref>\'\'Alpha Flight\'\' #33-36</ref> Namor, with the help of various superheroes including [[Alpha Flight]] and the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], freed Marrina and battled Attuma. Attuma ended the battle when he realized that Namor was happy with Marrina and did not want to return to Atlantis.<ref>\'\'Alpha Flight\'\' #39; \'\'The Avengers\'\' #272</ref> He did swear vengeance against the superheroes who had allied against him and joined the \'\'Lethal Legion\'\', a group of supervillains, shortly afterwards. The Legion was short-lived though and Attuma returned to Atlantis.  During the events of "[[The Evolutionary War]]," Attuma joined forces with the Lemurians and reserve Avengers against the [[High Evolutionary]].<ref>\'\'The Avengers\'\' Annual #17</ref>\n\nAs depicted in the [[Fictional crossover#Comics|crossover]] \'\'[[Atlantis Attacks]]\'\', Attuma allied himself with [[Ghaur]] and his [[Deviant (comics)|Deviants]] and [[Llyra]] and her [[Lemuria (comics)|Lemurians]] against humanity. He battled Iron Man and Namor, and then learned that the superhero Andromeda was his estranged daughter.<ref name="Iron Man Annual #10"/> Attuma mobilized his troops for an assault against the United States.<ref>\'\'The Avengers\'\' Annual #18</ref> He abducted [[Jean Grey|Marvel Girl]] as part of Ghaur\'s plan. Attuma then finally met his daughter Andromeda again, who now led a group of Atlantean rebels against him. Attuma fought his daughter and defeated her in personal combat, but Ghaur spirited her away.<ref name="X-Factor Annual #4"/> Attuma was angered, but continued with their plans. Ghaur and Llyra tricked him though and while Attuma attacked the surface world, battling [[Spider-Man]] and the Fantastic Four, Ghaur and Llyra attacked Atlantis and killed a large part of the population as a sacrifice to their god Set. When hearing about Atlantis\' destruction, Attuma went into a stupor and the Atlanteans retreated.<ref>\'\'Web of Spider-Man\'\' Annual #5</ref> Attuma was put on trial, but made his way back to Atlantis.\n\nShortly afterwards, Attuma briefly lost control of Atlantis and Namor returned to the throne, but Attuma regained the throne of Atlantis, when Namor was cursed as one of the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]], a curse which would transport Namor away whenever the Defenders were needed and leave Atlantis undefended. However, his strength, stamina, resistance to physical injury, and swimming speed are much higher than the majority of his race. Like most Atlanteans, Attuma could not survive out of water for more than 10 minutes without the aid of mechanical devices or certain chemicals, for most of his life. His stamina, agility, and reflexes also were reduced when out of the water. Like all Atlanteans his specially developed vision allows him to see clearly in the murky depths of the ocean.\n\nAfter certain genetic and surgical enhancements following his resurrection at the hands of [[Doctor Doom]] his strength has been fortified to be on par with the strength of [[Namor]] as well as granting him the ability of self-propelled flight and living on land unaided.\n\nAttuma is a skilled Atlantean warrior, trained in the use of many different weapons. He is an expert hand-to-hand combatant, especially underwater, and has achieved mastery in the use of most Atlantean weapons. He usually prefers his three-pronged iron sword, but he has access to highly advanced weaponry.\n\n==Other versions==\n\n===Marvel Adventures===\nIn this child-friendly continuity, Attuma becomes involved after the Invisible Woman is accused of stealing a precious Atlantean artifact.<ref>\'\'Marvel Adventures\'\' #24 (August 2007)</ref>\n\n===Marvel Zombies===\nA zombie Attuma makes an appearance in \'\'Dead Days\'\' on one panel fighting the [[Thing (comics)|Thing]] alongside Namor.<ref>\'\'Marvel Zombies: Dead Days\'\' #1 (July 2007)</ref> A zombie Namor from another universe fondly remembers finding Attuma and his forces in the Marinaras Trench and slaughtering them.<ref>\'\'Marvel Zombies Return\'\' #5 (2009)</ref>\n\n==In other media==\n\n===Television===\n* Attuma appeared in the \'\'Namor\'\' segment of \'\'[[The Marvel Super Heroes]]\'\'.{{cn|date=October 2020}}\n* Attuma appeared in the \'\'[[Fantastic Four (1967 TV series)|Fantastic Four]]\'\' episode "Danger in the Depths", voiced by [[Henry Corden]].<ref name="Behind the Voice Actors">[https://www.behindthevoiceactors.com/characters/Marvel-Universe/Attuma/ Voice(s) of "Attuma."] \'\'Behind The Voice Actors\'\'.</ref>\n* Attuma appears in \'\'[[The Avengers: United They Stand]]\'\' episode "To Rule Atlantis", voiced by [[Philip Akin]].<ref name="Behind the Voice Actors"/>\n* Attuma appears in the \'\'[[Fantastic Four: World\'s Greatest Heroes]]\'\' episode "Atlantis Attacks".{{cn|date=October 2020}}\n* Attuma appears in \'\'[[Avengers Assemble (TV series)|Avengers Assemble]]\'\',<ref>"[http://io9.com/5989494/first-look-at-disneys-new-avengers-assemble-cartoon First Look at Disney\'s New Avengers Assemble Cartoon], [[io9]].
