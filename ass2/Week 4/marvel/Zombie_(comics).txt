The initial modern story, co-scripted by Thomas and [[Steve Gerber]] and drawn by [[John Buscema]] and [[Tom Palmer (comics)|Tom Palmer]], was a 12-page tale that led into a seven-page reprint of the 1950s story (with the art slightly altered to give the Zombie shoulder-length rather than short hair).<ref>[http://www.comics.org/issue/26336/ \'\'Zombie\'\' #1 (Marvel, 1973)] at the [[Grand Comics Database]].</ref><ref>Trademarked cover title is \'\'Tales of the Zombie\'\'; copyrighted title in postal indicia is \'\'Zombie\'\' except for the fourth and 10th issues, copyrighted as \'\'Tales of the Zombie\'\', per [http://www.comics.org/series/2129/ \'\'Zombie\'\' (Marvel, 1973 Series)] at the Grand Comics Database.</ref> That original story was also reprinted in 1975\'s \'\'[[Tales of the Zombie|Tales of the Zombie Super Annual]]\'\' #1,<ref>[http://www.comics.org/issue/28073/ \'\'Tales of the Zombie Annual\'\' #1] at the Grand Comics Database.</ref> and again two decades later in \'\'Curse of the Weird\'\' #4 (March 1994), the final issue of a short-lived Marvel horror reprint series.\n\nFollowing the premiere, all the Zombie stories were by Gerber and artist [[Pablo Marcos]] (one of these in collaboration with writer [[Doug Moench]] and artist [[Alfredo Alcala]]). The original series\' finale, set at Garth\'s daughter\'s wedding in issue #9, was a three-chapter story written by [[Tony Isabella]] (chapter 2 with co-scripter [[Chris Claremont]]), and drawn by [[pencilers]] Virgilio Redondo, Yong Montano, and [[Ron Wilson (comics)|Ron Wilson]], respectively, and [[inker]] by Alcala (chapters 1–2) and Marcos (chapter 3).\n\n\'\'Tales of the Zombie\'\' published the last work of [[Golden Age of comic books|Golden Age]] great [[Syd Shores]], [[Captain America]]\'s first penciler following [[Jack Kirby]]\'s departure from the character in 1941. [[Dick Ayers]] penciled the remainder of writer [[Tony Isabella]]\'s anthological horror tale.\n\nAlthough laid to peaceful rest in \'\'Tales of the Zombie\'\' #9 (he did not appear in the following, final issue, which contained a [[Brother Voodoo]] story and three anthological tales), Simon Garth was reanimated in the horror-comics magazine \'\'Bizarre Adventures\'\' #33 (Dec. 1982), in an out-of-chronology story hard to reconcile with the remainder of the character\'s continuity. The Zombie returned to color comic books in a backup story in \'\'[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics series)|Daredevil Annual]]\'\' #9 (July 1993). At the time of that appearance, the Zombie remained unearthed, and controlled by Donna, who pledged to have him eliminate other such enthralled undead.\n\n[[File:Menace5 AtlasComics.jpg|thumb|left|\'\'[[Menace (Atlas Comics)|Menace]]\'\' #5 (July 1953), cover art by [[Bill Everett]].]]\nThe Zombie thereafter appeared in \'\'[[Peter Parker: Spider-Man]] Annual \'97\'\' (1997); in a behind-the-scenes reference in \'\'[[Blade (comics)|Blade]]: Crescent City Blues\'\' #1 (March 1998) leading into a guest appearance in \'\'[[Spider-Man Unlimited (comics)|Spider-Man Unlimited]]\'\' #20 (May 1998); and in a solo story in the anthology series \'\'[[Strange Tales]]\'\' (vol. 4) #1 (Sept. 1998). A decade later, he  starred in a solo story in the [[One-shot (comics)|one-shot]] omnibus \'\'[[Legion of Monsters]]: [[Man-Thing]]\'\' #1 (May 2007). Ambushed and kidnapped by his former [[gardener]] (whom he had fired), Garth is to be a [[Louisiana Voodoo|voodoo]] cult\'s [[human sacrifice]]. However, the cult\'s priestess Layla recognizes Garth as her own everyday-life employer, with whom she is in love. Though her attempt to let him escape is thwarted, and though she is forced to mystically transform his corpse into a [[zombie]] with a clouded mind whom holders of the matching amulet could control, Layla, with her grandfather, Papa Doc Kabel, continue to try to help the uncomprehending Zombie reach his final rest.\n\nDespite his zombie state, he retains some vestige of his soul: for instance, when under the control of the amulet he has been forced to hurt or even kill people he has come to care about (such as Philip Bliss and Layla). The moment he is free from control, his vengeance is terrible. Because of these remnants of soul, Layla and Papa Doc perform a ritual that allows Garth 24 hours in his restored human self in order that he might attend the wedding of his daughter Donna and set what was left of his previous life in order.<ref>\'\'Tales of the Zombie\'\' #8</ref>\n\nHe was resurrected by the [[Haitian Vodou|voodoo]] witch [[Calypso (comics)|Calypso]] who discovered that, through acts of [[Altruism|selflessness]], this particular zombie possessed free will, i.e. the ability to act of his own accord and not always at the request of whoever was wearing the Amulet of Damballah &mdash; an unusual feature for a member of the walking dead. In this case, he ignored her orders and pushed Calypso aside in order to release the captive soul of his friend Papa Doc Kabel, whom Calypso had murdered as part of the process of reviving Garth.<ref>\'\'Daredevil Annual\'\' #9 (July 1993)</ref>\n\n===\'\'Marvel Zombies 4\'\'===\n{{main|Marvel Zombies 4}}\nSimon Garth had been a test subject of [[A.R.M.O.R.]] when the zombie plague killed everyone on the base. He retrieved the head of zombie [[Deadpool]], and used the base\'s teleporters to escape to the bottom of the sea, where Deadpool\'s head infected all of the Men-Fish and their leader the [[Piranha (comics)|Piranha]]. Garth was compelled to travel to the island nation of Taino in the Caribbean Sea, where he told [[Black Talon (comics)#Black Talon (Samuel Barone)|Black Talon]] about the zombie plague. Black Talon assumed control of Garth, and captured Deadpool\'s head.<ref>\'\'Marvel Zombies 4\'\' #1</ref> When the zombie Deadpool\'s head bites one of the Black Talon\'s henchmen, he uses this opportunity to get Garth to help him escape.<ref>\'\'Marvel Zombies 4\'\' #2</ref> At the end of the series, [[Jennifer Kale]] and the [[Black Talon (comics)|Black Talon]] confine the zombie virus inside Garth.<ref>\'\'Marvel Zombies 4\'\' #4</ref>\n\nThe Zombie was later recruited by [[Phil Coulson]] to join his incarnation of the [[Howling Commandos]] in order to fight [[Dormammu]]\'s Mindless Plague.<ref>\'\'S.H.I.E.L.D.\'\' (vol. 3) #6</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nAs the Zombie, Garth is supernaturally strong and able to heal mystically from injuries.
