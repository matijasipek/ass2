Some time later, he exposed [[Captain Marvel (Mar-Vell)|Captain Marvel]] to a carcinogenic [[nerve gas]];<ref>\'\'Captain Marvel\'\' #34</ref> this encounter ultimately caused Captain Marvel\'s fatal cancer.<ref>"The Death of Captain Marvel"</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Brevoort |first1=Tom |last2=DeFalco |first2=Tom |last3=Manning |first3=Matthew K. |last4=Sanderson |first4=Peter |last5=Wiacek |first5=Win |title=Marvel Year By Year: A Visual History |date=2017 |publisher=DK Publishing |isbn=978-1465455505 |page=166}}</ref> Since then he has clashed with Earth\'s superhumans, who have found creative ways to defeat him, including using the "fractioning (separation) of his exploded mass" against him (essentially, if he can\'t reform himself after an explosion, he can\'t explode again).\n\nNitro later battled and was defeated by [[Omega the Unknown]].<ref>\'\'Omega the Unknown\'\' #8</ref> He later escaped from [[Features of the Marvel Universe#Project_Pegasus|Project: Pegasus]], and then battled and was defeated by [[Spider-Man]].<ref name=autogenerated1>\'\'Peter Parker, the Spectacular Spider-Man\'\' #55</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Cowsill |first1=Alan |last2=Manning |first2=Matthew K. |title=Spider-Man Chronicle: Celebrating 50 Years of Web-Slinging |date=2012 |publisher=DK Publishing |isbn=978-0756692360 |page=119}}</ref> Nitro was later freed from his containment canister by the [[Vulture (Marvel Comics)|Vulture]] in Albany, New York. After he exploded several times in a minute, he passed out from exhaustion (presumably, it takes energy to explode, and he could only spare so much before he passed out). The transformation and explosion to can be limited to discrete portions of his body, such as his fist, and aim the force in a specific direction. While in his gaseous state, he cannot reform if any fraction of his body is separated from the rest, and he must completely reform his body from a gaseous state before he can detonate a second time.
