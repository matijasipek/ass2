She is quite confused about why Odin took all of the Asgardians back to the Asgard Realm, and is still deciding as to whether she should join them. While saving some people in the city, she is attacked because she has a hammer similar to those which had been appearing all around the earth, and accidentally kills a police officer who shot at her by deflecting the bullets back into him. She left Earth for the stars.<ref>\'\'Fear Itself: Youth in Revolt\'\' #1-6</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nAs her powers are based upon the Asgardians, Thor Girl possesses the conventional physical attributes of the Asgardian gods. She is immune to all Earthly diseases and is highly durable, able to withstand attacks which would destroy an ordinary human. She also will heal at incredible speeds due to her godly life force. Thor Girl possesses superhuman strength which rivals the most powerful of the gods and her now Asgardian-level metabolism provides her with far greater than human endurance in all physical activities. Additionally, like most Asgardians, she is near-ageless with a lifetime which may stretch into several millennia.{{issue|date=July 2014}}\n\nAs Thor Girl, Tarene wields a gold hammer through which she could channel her powers as a continuous beam of energy capable of knocking Thor off his feet and shattering buildings. Like Thor, she can also use her hammer to fly and control the weather, allowing her to summon storms, gusts of wind, create lightning bolts and various other effects. She can also traverse dimensional barriers such as from Earth to Asgard.{{issue|date=July 2014}}\n\nWhen she had her cosmic powers, the full extent of what she could do was unknown, but it was stated that her powers were on a level that closely rivaled Odin\'s.{{Issue|date=July 2009}}\n\n==Other versions==\nIn one alternate future world, Thor Girl tried to reach back in time to empower the alien [[Desak]] trying to locate a missing Thor.<ref>\'\'Thor Annual 2001\'\'</ref><ref>\'\'Thor\'\' vol. 2 #48, 50</ref>\n\n==In other media==\n===Video games===\n* Thor Girl appears as a playable character in \'\'[[Lego Marvel\'s Avengers]]\'\' voiced by [[Kate Higgins]].\n\n==References==\n{{Reflist}}\n\n==External links==\n{{Portal|Speculative fiction|Comics}}\n*[http://Marvel.wikia.com/Tarene_%28Earth-616%29 Thor Girl] at Marvel Wikia\n\n{{Thor}}\n{{Avengers characters}}\n{{Initiative}}\n{{John Romita Jr.}}\n\n[[Category:Comics characters introduced in 2000]]\n[[Category:Characters created by John Romita Jr.]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics Asgardians]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics characters who use magic]]<!--Her hammer has magical properties-->\n[[Category:Marvel Comics characters with accelerated healing]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics characters with superhuman strength]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics extraterrestrial superheroes]]\n[[Category:Characters created by Dan Jurgens]]\n[[Category:Fictional characters with electric or magnetic abilities]]\n[[Category:Fictional characters with weather abilities]]\n[[Category:Fictional hammer fighters]]\n[[Category:Fictional women soldiers and warriors]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics female superheroes]]'}]}}}}
