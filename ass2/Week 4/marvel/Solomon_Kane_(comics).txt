He was originally created by the [[pulp magazine|pulp]]-era writer [[Robert E. Howard]]. [[Dark Horse Comics]] began publishing a new series of Kane stories in 2008, and also published collections of the 1970s Marvel stories in 2009.\n\n==Marvel Comics==\n[[Marvel Comics]] published several comic books featuring Solomon Kane. He was the lead character in the six-issue [[Limited series (comics)|limited series]] \'\'Sword of Solomon Kane\'\', published 1985-6. He also appeared numerous times in the company\'s black and white, non-[[Comics Code Authority|Code]] approved magazine format comics, most frequently in \'\'Savage Sword of Conan\'\', starring Howard\'s most popular pulp character. The complete list of Marvel\'s Solomon Kane story appearances are:\n \n* \'\'[[Monsters Unleashed (comics)|Monsters Unleashed]]\'\' #1 (August 1973), an adaptation of Howard\'s \'\'Skulls in the Stars\'\' by [[Roy Thomas]], art by [[Ralph Reese]].\n* \'\'[[Dracula Lives]]\'\' #3 (October 1973), Kane meets Dracula by Thomas, art by [[Alan Weiss (comics)|Alan Weiss]].\n* \'\'[[Conan (Marvel Comics)|The Conan Saga]]\'\' #50 (May 1991) by Alan Rowlands, art by Steve Carr and [[Al Williamson]] (although this magazine was a reprint title, the Kane story in this issue had not been previously published).\n* \'\'[[Kull of Atlantis#Comics|Kull and the Barbarians]]\'\' #2–3 (July and September 1975), an adaptation of \'\'The Hills of the Dead\'\' by Thomas, art by Weiss, [[Neal Adams]] and [[Pablo Marcos]].\n* \'\'[[Marvel Premiere]]\'\' #33–34 (December 1976 and February 1977). Like the Kane mini-series, this was a Code-approved four-color comic, an adaptation of \'\'Red Shadows\'\' by Thomas, art by [[Howard Chaykin]].\n* \'\'[[Marvel Preview]]\'\' #19 (Summer 1979), an adaptation of "The Footfalls Within" by [[Donald F. Glut|Don Glut]], art by [[Will Meugniot]] and [[Steve Gan]].\n* \'\'[[Savage Sword of Conan]]\'\' #13–14, 18–20, 22, 25, 26, 33, 34, 37, 39, 41, 53, 54, 62, 83, 162, 171, 219 and 220 (July 1976–April 1994) by Thomas, Glut, [[Doug Moench]], [[Jo Duffy]] and [[John Arcudi]], art by Gan, Chaykin, Weiss, and many others, including a sequel to the Kane vs. Dracula story, two adaptations of the Howard poem \'\'Solomon Kane\'s Homecoming\'\',<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20091026210008/http://www.geocities.com/ratmmjess/kane.html Jess Nevins\' Solomon Kane Comic Page].</ref> and in the last two issues, a meeting of Kane and Conan for which Thomas, with artist [[Colin MacNeil]], used Howard\'s brief Kane fragment, "Death\'s Black Riders," as a springboard.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.comics.org/details.lasso?id=174819 |title=Savage Sword of Conan #219|publisher=The Grand Comics Database |date= |accessdate=2010-10-05}}</ref>\n* \'\'The Sword of Solomon Kane\'\'<ref>The [[Indicia (publishing)|indicia]] calls it simply \'\'Solomon Kane\'\'</ref> #1–6 (September 1985–July 1986). Of the six issues, four adapted Howard stories (all previously adapted by Marvel), and two (#2 and #4) contained original stories by [[Ralph Macchio (comics)|Ralph Macchio]], with the art credits varying. The finale also contained yet another rendering of the poem "Solomon Kane\'s Homecoming," illustrated by [[Sandy Plunkett]] and Williamson.<ref>[http://www.solomonkane.com/sk.pdf Solomon Kane Info Page] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050819234736/http://www.solomonkane.com/sk.pdf |date=August 19, 2005 }}</ref>\n\n==Dark Horse Comics==\nIt was announced at the 2006 Comic Con that [[Paradox Entertainment]] has completed a publishing deal with [[Dark Horse Comics]] for a \'\'Solomon Kane\'\' comic series, to be written by [[Scott Allie]].<ref name=cbr>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=11252 Robert E. Howard\'s Solomon Kane Returns at Dark Horse], [[Comic Book Resources]], August 18, 2007</ref><ref>[http://www.newsarama.com/comics/080721-SolomonKane.html Scott Allie - Bringing Solomon Kane to Comic Book Life], [[Newsarama]], July 21, 2008</ref> The first two arcs featured completed versions of two Howard fragments — "The Castle of the Devil" and "Death\'s Black Riders." The first issue picks Kane up traveling through the Black Forest after ending his military career.<ref name=cbr /> It was a five-issue [[Limited series (comics)|mini-series]], based on "Castle of the Devil", and featured art by Mario Guevara (pencils), [[Dave Stewart (artist)|Dave Stewart]] (colors) and [[John Cassaday]] (covers). An eight-page sample was posted on the [[Dark Horse Presents]] [[MySpace]] page in June 2008 <ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=16110 NYCC Exclusive: Dark Horse Runs Wild with "Solomon Kane"], [[Comic Book Resources]], April 20, 2008</ref> and the first issue was published in September 2008.<ref>[http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=17169 EXCLUSIVE: Solomon Kane #1 Preview], [[Comic Book Resources]],  July 10, 2008</ref>\n\nThe second arc, "Death\'s Black Riders", was a four-issue mini-series published from January 2010 to June 2010.  The third arc, "Red Shadows," had its first issue published in April 2011.  "Red Shadows", based on a Robert E. Howard story and previously interpreted by Marvel in \'\'Marvel Premiere\'\' #33–34 (1976), follows Solomon Kane as he tracks le Loup, an expert French swordsman and womanizer with a habit of killing his conquests, through Europe, across the Mediterranean, and down the West Coast of Africa.\n\n==Collected editions==\n\'\'Solomon Kane Volume 1: The Castle of the Devil\'\', a [[trade paperback (comics)|trade paperback]] collection of the Dark Horse five-issue mini-series, was published in July 2009 ({{ISBN|1595822828}}).\n\nIn February 2009, Dark Horse announced a new collection entitled \'\'The Saga of Solomon Kane - Soft Cover\'\' ({{ISBN|1595823174}}) which collected the 1970s Marvel stories.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.darkhorse.com/Books/16-043/The-Saga-of-Solomon-Kane |title=Dark Horse - The Saga of Solomon Kane |accessdate=2009-04-20}}</ref>  This 400+ page collection includes various stories from \'\'Savage Sword of Conan\'\', \'\'Conan Saga\'\', \'\'Kull and the Barbarians\'\', \'\'Marvel Preview\'\', \'\'Monsters Unleashed\'\' and \'\'Dracula Lives\'\'.\n\nIn December 2009, Dark Horse announced a collection entitled \'\'Robert E.
