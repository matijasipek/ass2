Because I have to believe in something greater than myself." from All-New Inhumans #4</ref> is a cousin of king [[Black Bolt]] and a member of the Royal Family of the [[Inhumans]]. As is custom among the Inhumans, Gorgon was exposed to the [[Terrigen Mist]]s as a teenager, gaining increased strength, while his feet were transformed to [[hoof|hooves]], capable of generating highly destructive seismic waves. As an adult, Gorgon became Black Bolt\'s personal bodyguard and is responsible for training youths recently exposed to the [[Terrigen Mists]] in the use of their newly acquired powers and abilities.\n\nGorgon first leaves the Inhuman city of [[Attilan]] to rescue [[Medusa (comics)|Medusa]] (suffering from [[amnesia]] at the time), who became lost in the outside world. This leads to a battle with the [[Fantastic Four]], who rescued Medusa from the supervillain team the [[Frightful Four]].<ref>\'\'Fantastic Four\'\' #44 (Nov. 1965)</ref> After making contact with the outside world, Gorgon and the rest of the Royal Family have several encounters with the Fantastic Four over the years, seeking their aid against foes such as Black Bolt\'s brother [[Maximus (comics)|Maximus]],<ref>\'\'Fantastic Four\'\' #45-47 (Dec. 1965 – Feb. 1966); This goes badly as many human captives are slain by an impatient Inhuman named Jolen. This sparks a war between Earth and the Inhumans, and Gorgon is captured. He is subjected to the Terrigen Mists again. This amplifies Gorgon\'s powers and change him into a true beast with a lion-like head, claws, and uncontrollable rages.<ref name="Silent War">\'\'Silent War\'\' #1-6 (2006–2007)</ref> As with the other Inhumans, he leaves the Moon to assist them in taking over the Kree empire.<ref>\'\'War of Kings\'\' #1-4 (2009)</ref> He is later seen with his more humanoid appearance.<ref name="The Thanos Imperative #1">\'\'The Thanos Imperative\'\' #1 (2010)</ref>\n\nIn the \'\'Inhuman\'\' storyline, he is shot in the spine by NuHuman named Lineage, he is paralyzed waist down and cannot use his powers.<ref>\'\'Inhuman\'\' #13</ref>\n\nAs part of the \'\'[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]\'\', Gorgon joined the diplomatic mission created by Medusa to oversee the diplomatic relations and handle the recovery efforts of the newly transformed Inhumans. He also possesses enhanced strength and durability, although like all Inhumans he has a weaker immune system than that of an average human.\n\nGorgon was exposed to Terrigen Mists a second time by Pentagon officials.
