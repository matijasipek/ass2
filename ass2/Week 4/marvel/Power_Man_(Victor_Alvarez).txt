He is the son of Reina Alvarez and [[Shades (comics)|Shades]]. As a child, he was caught in an explosion caused by the villain [[Bullseye (comics)|Bullseye]] that resulted in the deaths of over 100 people including his father. Victor survived by somehow using a technique that drew the [[Qi|chi]] from the dead bodies around him and temporarily granted him superhuman strength and fortitude.<ref>\'\'Shadowland: Power Man\'\' #3</ref>\n\nSome time later during the \'\'[[Shadowland (comics)|Shadowland]]\'\' storyline, Victor took on the name Power Man and began using his abilities to fight crime as a hero for hire, advertising his services on sites like [[Craigslist]]. Victor\'s activities eventually drew the attention of [[Luke Cage]], a member of the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] who had once used the Power Man name. Cage and his partner [[Iron Fist (comics)|Iron Fist]] eventually learn that Victor is the son of Shades, a Puerto Rican supervillain that Cage had fought years earlier. Though Victor dislikes both Cage and Iron Fist, he eventually teams up with them to help the other heroes fight the crazed [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Matt Murdock]] and his army of [[The Hand (comics)|Hand]] ninjas.<ref>\'\'Shadowland: Power Man\'\' #1-4</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=user_review&id=2587|title=Shadowland: Power Man #1|date=23 August 2010}}</ref>\n\nDuring the \'\'[[Fear Itself (comics)|Fear Itself]]\'\' storyline, Power Man ends up teleported onto a station in the middle of the Pacific Ocean with [[Amadeus Cho]], [[Anya Corazon|Spider-Girl]], [[Thunderstrike (Kevin Masterson)|Thunderstrike]] and [[X-23]].
