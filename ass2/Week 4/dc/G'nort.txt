The fact that the region was uninhabited also suited the needs of the Manhunters, who set up their home planet in G\'Nort\'s sector. Also an idiot, the Skier was mistaken for a pan-handling homeless person when traveling in the subway.\n\nG\'nort goes on a mission to rescue [[Mister Miracle]] from the interstellar trader [[Manga Khan]]. Traveling in a spaceship, G\'nort works with [[Big Barda]], [[Martian Manhunter]], and Dmitri of the [[Rocket Reds]]. They chase down Khan and survive an assassination attempt by his hired killer [[Lobo (DC Comics)|Lobo]].<ref>\'\'Justice League International\'\' #14-16 (June–August 1988)</ref>\n\nBoth G\'nort and the Skier were founding members of Justice League Antarctica, created by [[Maxwell Lord]] for the purpose of keeping both them and the reformed [[Injustice League]] out of his way.\n\nThe League, including G\'nort, find their Antarctic headquarters overwhelmed by killer penguins. Their enemies are ultimately destroyed, but not before the facility itself gets demolished. G\'Nort keeps himself and his new-found allies alive via his power-ring until help arrives.<ref>\'\'Justice League America\'\' Annual #4 (1990)</ref>\n\nG\'nort assists the League, this time consisting of Manhunter, [[Crimson Fox]] and [[Ice (comics)|Ice]], when Mr. Nebula, the Skier\'s old boss, turns his dangerous, cosmic-powered attention to [[Earth]]. A race of clowns, the Poglachians gave the rings to beings they thought would use them in amusing ways. They were actually pawns of the Weaponers of [[Qward]], who hoped to discredit the Green Lantern Corps by having the name associated with idiots.
