He also invented a bullet-shaped Gravity Regulator Helmet which allowed him to fly and deflect bullets.<ref name=Benton>{{cite book |last1=Benton |first1=Mike |title=Superhero Comics of the Golden Age: The Illustrated History |date=1992 |publisher=Taylor Publishing Company |location=Dallas |isbn=0-87833-808-X |url=https://archive.org/details/superherocomicso0000bent/page/80 |access-date=1 April 2020 |pages=81–82}}</ref> His first case involved capturing a gangster in his hideout.<ref name="dc-ency2">{{Citation | last = Greenberger | first = Robert | author-link = Robert Greenberger | contribution = Bulletman | editor-last = Dougall | editor-first = Alastair | title = The DC Comics Encyclopedia | pages = 63 | publisher = [[Dorling Kindersley]] | place = London | year = 2008 | isbn = 978-0-7566-4119-1}}</ref> He has fought the supercriminal Blackmask, the rat-man Black Rat and the devious Mara Myle.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Nevins |first1=Jess |title=Encyclopedia of Golden Age Superheroes |date=2013 |publisher=High Rock Press |isbn=978-1-61318-023-5 |page=41}}</ref>\n\nShortly after Bulletman began his crime-fighting career, he created a second helmet for his girlfriend and later wife Susan Kent, daughter of Police Sergeant Kent who adopted the name \'\'\'Bulletgirl\'\'\'.<ref>\'\'Master Comics\'\' #13 (April 1941)</ref><ref name="dc-ency1">{{Citation | last = Greenberger | first = Robert | author-link = Robert Greenberger | contribution = Bulletgirl I & II | editor-last = Dougall | editor-first = Alastair | title = The DC Comics Encyclopedia | pages = 63 | publisher = [[Dorling Kindersley]] | place = London | year = 2008 | isbn = 978-0-7566-4119-1}}</ref> His formula was shown to make him strong enough to smash a tank. In addition to being bullet-shaped, their helmets also make the characters bulletproof.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Morris |first1=Jon |title=The Legion of Regrettable Supervillains: Oddball Criminals from Comic Book History |date=2017 |publisher=Quirk Books |isbn=978-1594749322 |page=63}}</ref> The helmet is shown to be so strong that once when Bulletgirl was run over by a Nazi tank she was only knocked out.\n\nAfter Bulletgirl\'s arrival, the pair fought a variety of weird, supernatural-themed villains including the Unholy Three, the Son of Dracula, the Man Without a Face, the Black Spider and the Limping Mummy.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Mitchell |first1=Kurt |last2=Thomas |first2=Roy |title=American Comic Book Chronicles: 1940–1944 |date=2019 |publisher=TwoMorrows Publishing |isbn=978-1605490892 |pages=80–81}}</ref> They also fought criminal fashion plate the Dude.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Morris |first1=Jon |title=The Legion of Regrettable Supervillains: Oddball Criminals from Comic Book History |date=2017 |publisher=Quirk Books |isbn=978-1594749322 |pages=62–65}}</ref>\n\nBulletman and Bulletgirl were [[Fawcett Comics]]\' second-most-popular characters after [[Captain Marvel (DC Comics)|Captain Marvel]] and the [[Marvel Family]]. They were leased by [[DC Comics]] along with the rest of Fawcett Comics\' stable of characters in 1972. However, the characters lapsed into public domain prior to the said acquisition, which later allowed [[AC Comics]] to reprint their Golden Age adventures.\n\nDuring this period of time, Bulletman and Bulletgirl appeared with fellow Fawcett heroes to form Shazam\'s [[Squadron of Justice]] against the forces of [[King Kull (DC Comics)|King Kull]], who was trying to wipe out humanity on all three Earths after capturing all the beings who gave the Marvels their powers. Bulletman and Bulletgirl battled villains on Earth-S, the Earth-2 [[Joker (comics)|Joker]], [[Weeper (DC Comics)|Weeper]], [[Shade (comics)|Shade]], and [[Doctor Light (Arthur Light)|Doctor Light]]. Their age appeared to be as it was in the 1940s without explanation. They subsequently appeared a few times with Captain Marvel and his family.\n\nBulletman and Bulletgirl were eventually retrofitted into DC Universe continuity, and have been speculated to be members of the [[All-Star Squadron]], although this has not been confirmed in a printed comic. For example, it is established that during World War II, Bulletman met the long-lived [[Green Lantern]] [[Abin Sur]]. In issues #39–40 of \'\'[[Starman (comics)|Starman]]\'\' James Barr is accused of being a traitor to the United States. He is seemingly implicated in the Nazi attack on the \'\'Normandie\'\' ocean liner in New York. This contradicts what is known by [[Starman (Jack Knight)|Jack Knight]]\'s father [[Starman (Ted Knight)|Ted Knight]], whose life Barr saved in Alaska, on the day of the \'\'Normandie\'\' attack. Constrained by promises of confidentiality to the same government that is prosecuting him, Barr goes on the run with Ted while [[Minute-Man]] tries to get the secret oath rescinded. Captain Marvel, currently much younger than Bulletman (than he was back then) fights with Jack Knight in an attempt to arrest Barr. Captain Marvel fails in his mission against Barr, partly because Jack is backed up by human police officers.\n\n===Windshear===\nIn \'\'[[The Power of Shazam!]]\'\' #32 (November 1997), James Barr and Susan Kent-Barr were given a daughter named Deanna Barr, who donned her mother\'s helmet and operated briefly under the name Windshear (not to be confused with the [[Marvel Comics]] [[Windshear (comics)|superhero of the same name]]). She teamed up with her father on his last adventure, to rescue [[Billy Batson]], [[Mary Bromfield]] and [[Freddy Freeman]] from the villain Chain Lightning.<ref name="dc-ency1"/>\n\n===Bulleteer===\n{{main article|Bulleteer}}\nIn 2005, a new Bulletgirl known as [[Bulleteer]] was introduced as one of the Seven in [[Grant Morrison]]\'s \'\'[[Seven Soldiers of Victory]]\'\'. She is unrelated to these older heroes, though her costume is to some extent inspired by theirs. In issue #3, Susan Barr appeared in a brief cameo visiting her successor.\n\n===Infinite Crisis===\n{{main article|Infinite Crisis}}\nBulletman and Bulletgirl later re-appeared in the pages of \'\'[[Infinite Crisis]]\'\' on the newly reborn Earth-S along with the other Fawcett superheroes.\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nAfter injecting themselves with a chemical, Bulletman and Bulletgirl gained superhuman strength and intelligence.
