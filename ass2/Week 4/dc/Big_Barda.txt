[[Mister Miracle|Scott Free]], Darkseid\'s adopted son, and, sensing a peace about him, falls in love.<ref name="dc-ency"/>\n\nBarda risks her own safety to work with the rebel cell led by the New God [[Himon]]. This comes directly after the murder of most of Himon\'s cell by the local authority, Wonderful Williki. This includes one of Barda\'s people, Auralie, who is tortured to death for dancing, a crime on Apokolips. Moments later, Himon kills Willik with a bomb. Barda, her Furies, Himon and Metron help Scott escape Apokolips. Darkseid himself calls for Scott to return but doesn\'t actually stop him, as Scott\'s escape can reignite his war with New Genesis. Barda, not emotionally prepared to escape herself, stays behind.<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' #9 (September 1972)</ref> Eventually, Barda turns her back on Granny Goodness and comes to Earth.<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' #4 (September/October 1971)</ref> Once there, she finds that Scott has taken up the mantel of the escape artist Thaddeus Brown, AKA Mister Miracle, and has teamed with his diminutive manager [[Oberon (comics)|Oberon]]. Oberon and Barda become close friends. For a while, Barda\'s Furies assist Scott in his showmanship efforts<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' #14 (July 1973)</ref> but they return to Apokolips by themselves. Scott and Barda are married by Scott\'s birth father, the [[Highfather]] of [[New Genesis]].<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' #18 (February/March 1974)</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Sacks |first1=Jason |last2=Dallas |first2=Keith |title=American Comic Book Chronicles: The 1970s |date=2014 |publisher=TwoMorrows Publishing |isbn=978-1605490564 |page=144}}</ref>\n\nFor a number of years Barda follows Scott and Oberon on tour. Eventually they retire from superheroing and move to Bailey, New Hampshire.<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' vol. 2, #1 (January 1989)</ref> Despite their best attempts, a normal life eludes them. Disasters plague them and eventually Barda, Scott, and Oberon leave Bailey to move into a loft in [[Greenwich Village]]. Barda forms a defense-training program for women called the New Female Furies.\n\n===Justice League===\nThough naïve regarding Earth customs, Barda relishes her roles of wife and housekeeper, but when duty calls she never hesitates to assume the posture of a warrior; and when Scott joins the [[Justice League]], Barda participates in several missions.<ref>\'\'Justice League International\'\' #7 (November 1987); \'\'Justice League America\'\' #28 (July 1989)</ref> In one instance, while training [[Fire (comics)|Fire]], her weapon, the Mega-Rod, is stolen from her car. With the assistance of her husband and the [[Huntress (Helena Bertinelli)|Huntress]], she gets it back, but not before many innocent people are killed by its wielder, who was unable to resist the corrupting influence of the Apokoliptian technology. Barda also led a JLA mission to rescue her husband after he was lost in space. The mission was a result of [[Manga Khan]] selling Scott to [[Granny Goodness]]. Her teammates included [[Martian Manhunter]], [[Dimitri Pushkin|Rocket Red]], and [[G\'nort]]. In an attempt to dissuade their successful pursuit, Manga Khan hired [[Lobo (DC Comics)|Lobo]], paying him in [[dolphin]] feed, to kill them. He almost does, before Barda teleports him to a random location, that being ten feet behind [[Guy Gardner (comics)|Guy Gardner]]\'s skull. The repercussions of that literal meeting of the minds would last for years.\n\nFor a time, Barda mourns Scott, believing him slain in a battle with [[Despero]], but it was a robot double that was destroyed, as part of a plan by Manga Khan.<ref>\'\'Justice League America\'\' vol. 2, #37-40 (April–July 1990)</ref>\n\nAfter their time with the JLI, the two leave Earth for [[New Genesis]],<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' vol. 2, #28 (June 1991)</ref> but, they soon return<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' vol. 3, #1-2 (April–May 1996)</ref> and take up temporary residence aboard the Justice League Refuge. During this period, the couple separate briefly due to Scott\'s lack of consideration for her feelings. Barda perishes, but is reborn via Scott\'s temporary access to great cosmic power.<ref>\'\'Mister Miracle\'\' vol. 3, #4-5 (June–July 1996)</ref>\n\nBarda has served as a member of the JLA in her own right as well. At [[Takion]]\'s order, she and fellow New God [[Orion (comics)|Orion]] were sent as agents of New Genesis to serve on the team. Takion predicted that the Earth faced a grave threat.<ref>\'\'JLA\'\' #17 (April 1998)</ref> Their mission is to help mobilize Earth\'s heroes against the omnipotent Mageddon. Over time, they become involved with several other JLA missions. [[Adam Strange]], needing help with an alien invasion, enslaves the JLA as part of a bluff. The League works for days, turning the planet into a giant teleportation beam. The aliens are sent off to the prison planet of Takron-Galtos, which Barda had mentioned several times during the ordeal. In another instance, Barda is badly wounded fighting the [[Queen Bee (comics)|Queen Bee]], a member of the newly reformed [[Injustice League]]. Once Mageddon is defeated, Barda and Orion resign from the JLA.<ref name="dc-ency"/><ref>\'\'JLA\'\' #41 (May 2000)</ref>\n\n===After the League===\nBarda and Scott then reside in the suburbs of [[Connecticut]] and are active adventurers.<ref>\'\'Orion\'\' #25 (June 2002)</ref> Barda never hesitates to lend a hand to her friends when her power and expertise are needed.\n\nBarda accompanies [[Batman]], [[Superman]], and [[Wonder Woman]] to Apokolips to rescue [[Supergirl]] from [[Darkseid]]\'s clutches,<ref>\'\'Superman/Batman\'\' #11-12 (August–September 2004)</ref> and accepts an invitation from [[Barbara Gordon|Oracle]] to become the heavy-hitter on the [[Birds of Prey (comics)|Birds of Prey]].<ref>\'\'Birds of Prey\'\' #100 (January 2007)</ref>\n\nA similar-looking character named [[Little Barda]] appeared as a member of the [[Teen Titans]] in \'\'[[52 (comics)|52]]\'\' #21; the character leaves the group in the same issue. Although the fight is long and continues in the midst of other larger concerns for her team, it ends in a draw.\n\nBig Barda is killed in the first issue of \'\'[[Death of the New Gods]]\'\'; her funeral occurs in the second issue of the series. This gives Barda a level of strength roughly parallel to Wonder Woman as well as a high resistance to injury approaching invulnerability. She is similarly resistant to disease and most toxins. As a former Female Fury trained by Granny Goodness, Barda is a master combatant in terms of swordsmanship and raw brawling, which - in conjunction with her raw strength and grit - make her an immediate match for Wonder Woman.<ref name="jla">{{Cite book | last=Beatty | first=Scott | title=JLA: The Ultimate Guide to the Justice League of America | publisher=Dorling Kindersley | date=November 2002 | isbn=0-7513-4798-1}}</ref><ref name="dc-ency"/>\n\nIn combat, Barda wears Apokoliptian battle armor, which enhances her already impressive durability. In addition, Barda manages a high-tech weapon, called the "Mega-Rod". This weapon can create boom tubes for teleportation over long distances, propel her high into the air and release energy blast capable of bringing down such powerful beings as Superman. For a limited time the Mega-Rod can also increase the gravity force of an individual.\n\n==Other versions==\n===\'\'Kingdom Come\'\'===\nBarda appears in the limited series \'\'[[Kingdom Come (comics)|Kingdom Come]]\'\' by [[Mark Waid]] and [[Alex Ross]], which is set in an alternate future timeline of DC continuity.
