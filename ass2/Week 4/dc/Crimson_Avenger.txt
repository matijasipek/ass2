Elwood did mention that he had "taken the name of a former lawman", meaning the by-then long defunct original Crimson Avenger.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Fleisher |first1=Michael L. |title=The Original Encyclopedia of Comic Book Heroes, Volume Three: Superman |date=2007 |publisher=DC Comics |isbn=978-1-4012-1389-3 |page=48}}</ref>\n\nAfter the introduction of the DC Comics multiverse in the 1960s, the original Crimson Avenger (Lee Travis) was explained to have lived on Earth-Two; Albert Elwood\'s Earth has never been specified.\n\n===Jill Carlyle===\nA female Crimson Avenger first appeared in \'\'[[Stars and S.T.R.I.P.E.]]\'\' #9 (April 2000), created by [[Geoff Johns]] and [[Scott Kolins]]. This version, like the original [[El Diablo (comics)|El Diablo]], served as a minor [[Spectre (comics)|Spirit of Vengeance]]. She was an African-American woman who possessed the powers of teleportation and intangibility. In a flashback sequence, it was revealed that Carlyle studied law but apparently lost a case in which the defendant was clearly guilty. She obtained a pair of [[Colt\'s Manufacturing Company|Colt]] pistols originally owned by the first Crimson Avenger and used them to exact vengeance upon the unknown criminal.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Flint |first1=Hanna |title=10 fierce superheroes the goddess Beyoncé should play |url=https://www.syfy.com/syfywire/10-fierce-superheroes-the-goddess-beyonce-should-play |website=SYFY WIRE |access-date=29 October 2020 |language=en |date=24 April 2018}}</ref> These guns are cursed such that, if the possessor uses them out of revenge, he or she will be cursed to track and kill those who have taken an innocent life.
