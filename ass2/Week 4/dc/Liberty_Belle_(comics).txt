Two are from [[DC Comics]]: Libby Lawrence and Jesse Chambers, and the other is from [[Charlton Comics]]: Caroline Dean.\n\n==DC Comics==\n===Libby Lawrence===\nThe first Liberty Belle was \'\'\'Libby Lawrence\'\'\'.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Markstein |first1=Don |website=Don Markstein\'s Toonopedia |access-date=2 April 2020  |url=http://www.toonopedia.com/libertyb.htm |title=Liberty Belle}}</ref> Her powers of enhanced speed, strength, and stamina were linked to the ringing of the [[Liberty Bell]] in [[Philadelphia]]. Early in her mystery-woman career, she had an arrangement with Tom Revere, a guard at the Liberty Bell. When she signaled him, he would ring the bell for her and trigger her powers.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Thomas |first1=Roy |title=The All-Star Companion: Vol 2 |date=2006 |publisher=TwoMorrows Publishing |isbn=978-1893905375 |page=88}}</ref> In her later years, after decades of super-strength many began to theorize about the nature of her powers. Some believed the sonic vibrations of the bell triggered a metahuman gene, some believed that it was mystical because Libby is connected to the power of the \'\'Spirit of America\'\', like the heroes [[Uncle Sam (comics)|Uncle Sam]] and [[General Glory]]. Most of Liberty Belle\'s heroic exploits took place during the Second World War, and she was one of the founding members (and later chairwoman) of the [[All-Star Squadron]].<ref name=War>{{cite book |last1=Green |first1=Paul |title=Encyclopedia of Weird War Stories: Supernatural and Science Fiction Elements in Novels, Pulps, Comics, Film, Television, Games and Other Media |date=2017 |publisher=McFarland & Co |isbn=978-1476666723 |page=113}}</ref> In her public identity, she was the famous radio columnist Libby Lawrence, and therefore well-known both in and out of costume. During the war, she married speedster [[Johnny Quick (Johnny Chambers)|Johnny Quick]]. After the war, they had a daughter, Jesse, who shared both their powers and took the codename [[Jesse Chambers|Jesse Quick]]. Libby Lawrence is a [[kinship and descent|descendant]] of Bess Lynn, alias Miss Liberty.\n\n====Publication history====\nThis character appeared in several publications.\nLiberty Belle debuted in \'\'[[Boy Commandos]]\'\' #1 (Winter 1942/1943). After her second appearance in \'\'Boy Commandos\'\' #2 (Spring 1943), the character thereafter appeared in \'\'[[Star Spangled Comics]]\'\' #20 (May 1943) through to \'\'Star Spangled Comics\'\' #68 (May 1947).<ref>{{cite book |last1=Benton |first1=Mike |title=Superhero Comics of the Golden Age: The Illustrated History |date=1992 |publisher=Taylor Publishing Company |location=Dallas |isbn=0-87833-808-X |url=https://archive.org/details/superherocomicso0000bent |url-access=registration |access-date=8 April 2020 |page=[https://archive.org/details/superherocomicso0000bent/page/184 184]}}</ref>\n\nIn 1981, DC published issue #1 of \'\'All-Star Squadron\'\', a book whose first story appeared as an insert in \'\'[[Justice League of America]]\'\' #193. The self-described "conceptualizer" of the book was its writer [[Roy Thomas]]. He chose to include Liberty Belle in this World War Two comic to, in his own words, "play down the Earth-Two heroes who have counterparts on Earth-One in favor of other, quite promising characters who have been ignored or underplayed".<ref>\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #1</ref> Thomas also said that Liberty Belle was chosen to stand in for [[Wonder Woman]]. \'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' lasted sixty-seven issues with the last being published in 1987.\n\nAs he did with several other characters, Roy Thomas took some liberties with Liberty Belle\'s golden age continuity by having her retroactively marry [[Johnny Quick]] in 1942, while all her golden age appearances throughout the 1940s depict her as a single woman with a semi-romantic relationship with Captain Rick Cannon of G2 Army Intelligence. Thomas also wrote a story depicting the death of Tom Revere in 1942, despite the fact that he was a supporting character from the golden age Liberty Belle stories, most of which take place in 1943 and later. Finally, Thomas had Liberty Belle retroactively wear a mask from her first appearance, rather than her original unmasked look, in which she disguised herself only by allowing her loosed hair to partly drape over her face in a look inspired by [[Veronica Lake]].\n\n\'\'[[Young All-Stars]]\'\' #1 came out in 1987 as a replacement book for \'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' in the wake of the \'\'[[Crisis on Infinite Earths]]\'\' and the loss of Earth-Two as a fictional setting. Liberty Belle was used as a supporting character in this book which came to an end in 1989 with issue #31.\n\nIn 1992 DC began publishing \'\'[[Justice Society of America]]\'\' with writer [[Len Strazewski]]. Liberty Belle appeared in [[Flashback (narrative)|flashbacks]] and only in her public persona of Libby Lawrence. She was used to flesh out the characters of Johnny Chambers (her ex-husband) and Jesse Chambers (their daughter).\n\nLiberty Belle was ranked 63rd in \'\'[[Comics Buyer\'s Guide]]\'s\'\' "100 Sexiest Women in Comics" list.<ref>{{cite book| last = Frankenhoff| first = Brent| author-link = Brent Frankenhoff| title = Comics Buyer\'s Guide Presents: 100 Sexiest Women in Comics| publisher = [[Krause Publications]] | year = 2011| page = 43| isbn = 978-1-4402-2988-6}}</ref>\n\n====Fictional character history====\n===== Early history =====\n\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #61 (September 1986) is the first true post-\'\'Crisis\'\' issue of that comic which chronicled the origin of Liberty Belle.\n\nLibby Lawrence wins the American Intercollegiate Girls Athletic Tournament and receives a bell-shaped medal made from a piece of the original [[Liberty Bell]]. Years later, she becomes a member of the American team at the [[1936 Summer Olympics|1936 Olympics]] in [[Berlin]], where she wins gold medals for competitive [[swimming (sport)|swimming]].<ref>\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #61 (September 1986)</ref>\n\n=====World War II=====\nWhen the [[World War II|Second World War]] begins in 1939, the young Libby Lawrence is in [[Poland]] acting as personal secretary to her father, Major James Lawrence. Her father is killed in a Nazi air raid and with the help of Rick Cannon, Libby begins a journey across Nazi occupied Europe gathering important intelligence along the way. At Dunkirk she swims the English Channel to bring the information to the Allies. Libby is received with a ticker-tape parade on New York\'s Fifth Avenue and is shortly thereafter given work at a daily news syndicate, and then as a radio reporter, and she even has a television program (suspended only after the events at [[Pearl Harbor]]).<ref name="auto">\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #61 (September 1986)</ref>\n\nIn the autumn of 1941, Libby is living in her hometown of [[Philadelphia]]. She rescues Rick Cannon, whom she thought to be a spy himself, from [[Nazism|Nazi]] saboteurs.<ref name="auto"/>\n\nNow a mystery-woman, Liberty Belle becomes involved in the founding of the All-Star Squadron. On December 8, 1941 she is present, with a handful of other mystery-men, in the [[White House]] when [[Franklin D. Roosevelt|President Roosevelt]] announces his order that the [[Justice Society of America]] mobilize all American costumed heroes into a single unit, an [[All-Star Squadron]], responsible directly to the President. Their first mission is to fly to the West Coast, search out any saboteurs and prevent a [[Japan]]ese attack on the US mainland.<ref>\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #1 (1981)</ref> They find the JSA have been captured by the time travelling villain [[Per Degaton]], who is planning to change the events in an attempt at world domination. They stopped this, though when Per Degaton went back to 1947 they forgot his involvement. Soon after, the core members of the All-Star Squadron, including Libby, begin the process of electing other mystery-men for membership. During the meeting, the matter of electing a permanent chairman is decided by secret ballot, and Liberty Belle receives the majority vote.<ref>\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #13 (1982)</ref>\n\nDays later, Libby returns to Philadelphia to visit with her friend Tom Revere. [[Independence Hall]] is attacked by [[Baron Blitzkrieg]], [[Zyklon (comics)|Zyklon]] and Major Zwerg. In the battle, Tom Revere is killed while Major Zwerg is badly injured, the two other Nazi agents escape with the Liberty Bell.<ref>\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #45 & #61</ref> After learning that Blitzkrieg wanted the Liberty Bell to restore his sight, Libby gets to her base in a church with the help of [[Hawkgirl]]. Lightning strikes the Bell, the Baron regains his sight and escapes and Libby gains the ability to manipulate sound waves.<ref>\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #46 & #61</ref>\n \nOn April 1, 1942 Libby marries Johnny Chambers in Boston.<ref>\'\'All-Star Squadron\'\' #50 (October 1985)</ref>\n\nLibby continues to lead the All-Star Squadron in DC\'s series published in the 1980s but set during World War II: \'\'[[All-Star Squadron]]\'\' and \'\'[[Young All-Stars]]\'\'.\n\n=====Later history=====\nAt some point after the War, Libby retires as a mystery-woman. Later still, she and Johnny have a child whom they name Jesse and begin raising a family.<ref>\'\'Justice Society of America\'\' #3 (1992)</ref>\n\nLibby finally breaks up with Johnny as he founds his company called \'\'Quick-Start\'\'; the divorce soon follows. It is during the painful divorce that daughter Jesse uses her father\'s speed formula for the first time.<ref>\'\'Justice Society of America\'\' #3 & #7 (1992)</ref>\n\nLibby later becomes extremely bitter about the costume-adventurer business.\n\nLibby puts on her costume again during the \'\'[[Infinite Crisis]]\'\'. Libby is fighting against [[Baron Blitzkrieg]] and other members of [[Secret Society of Super Villains|the Society]] when her powers fail. She goes to Philadelphia and attempts to increase her power levels by repeatedly ringing the Liberty Bell. However this does not work, and Belle is trapped on the bell which is producing damage via sonic waves. The JSA try to help her and in the end she is saved by [[Courtney Whitmore|Stargirl]].<ref>\'\'Justice Society of America\'\' #81 (April 2006)</ref>\n\nA short time after the events of the \'\'[[Brightest Day]]\'\' crossover, Libby returns to superheroics after being recruited by [[Manhunter (Kate Spencer)|Manhunter]] to help the JSA battle a villain named [[Scythe (comics)|Scythe]].<ref>\'\'Justice Society of America\'\' #48</ref> Writer [[Marc Guggenheim]] explained that having Libby return to the Liberty Belle identity was suggested to him by [[James Robinson (comics)|James Robinson]] after Libby\'s daughter returned to the Jesse Quick identity and joined the [[Justice League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newsarama.com/comics/justice-society-marc-guggenheim-110323.html |title=JUSTICE SOCIETY &#124; Marc Guggenheim &#124; JUSTICE SOCIETY Gets New Characters, More Humor with #50 |publisher=Newsarama.com |access-date=2011-04-11}}</ref>\n\nIn the pages of "[[Dark Nights: Death Metal]]", Libby is revealed to be entombed in the Valhalla Cemetery.<ref>\'\'Dark Nights: Death Metal\'\' #2. DC Comics.</ref>\n\n===Jesse Chambers===\n{{main|Jesse Chambers}}\nJesse Chambers is the second woman in the [[DC Universe]] to take the name of Liberty Belle. As discussed earlier, she is the daughter of Liberty Belle and Johnny Quick, having inherited her mother\'s powers. She combines those powers of enhanced strength, agility, reflexes and stamina with her father\'s speed, which is invoked by reciting a mathematical formula. Realizing her powers, she joins the [[Justice Society of America|JSA]] in its third incarnation. Although originally known as the speedster \'\'Jesse Quick\'\', in December 2006 she debuted as Liberty Belle. Jesse marries the second [[Hourman (Rick Tyler)|Hourman]], Rick Tyler, and they work as a duo within the Justice Society, until the team splits into a smaller Justice Society and a new incarnation of the All-Stars. In 2010, she joins the [[Justice League of America]].\n\n==Charlton Comics==<!-- This section is linked from [[Steve Ditko]] -->\nA backup feature in [[Charlton Comics|Charlton]]\'s \'\'[[E-Man]]\'\' #5 (November 1974), \'\'\'Caroline Dean\'\'\', the second Liberty Belle, was a red-white-and-blue heroine who helps out on an American rocket launch.
