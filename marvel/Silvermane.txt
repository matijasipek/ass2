He first crossed paths with Connors\' alter-ego, the Lizard, and [[Spider-Man]] during this scheme. Drinking the serum made Silvermane younger, but its effects caused him to de-age further and further until he was a infant before completely disappearing.<ref>\'\'[[The Amazing Spider-Man]]\'\' #73-75. marvel Comics.</ref> Silvermane eventually reappeared, revealing that he had mystically aged to his forties. He started a new career and again quickly rose through the Maggia\'s ranks until he regained control of his family, the sought to form an alliance with [[Hydra (comics)|Hydra]], determined to take over the world as their leader, the Supreme Hydra. Marvel Comics.</ref> Silvermane reappeared in New York in an attempt to unite all of New York\'s gangs under his leadership and take over the criminal underworld, but his plans were complicated by the [[Green Goblin#Bart Hamilton|Green Goblin]], who opposed him. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nSilvermane has worked with many super-villains, such as [[Hammerhead (comics)|Hammerhead]], and he has also been known to be a rival of the [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin]], even once trying to assassinate the crime lord while he was suffering from amnesia.<ref>\'\'The Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #197. He was a superb hand-to-hand combatant, an excellent marksman, and a brilliant strategist and organizer. The use of magical potions temporarily granted him a form of immortality, allowing him to appear as a man in his early-to-mid 40s. His preferred weapons included various handguns and the Thompson .45 caliber machine gun. Later, Silvermane\'s brain, vital organs, and head were transplanted into a cyborg body, increasing his physical abilities and granting him superhuman strength and senses. However, Silvermane\'s remaining organic body parts are those of an elderly man, and thus extremely vulnerable.\n\n==Other versions==\n===Ultimate Marvel===\nThe [[Ultimate Marvel]] version of Silvermane had a brief cameo.
