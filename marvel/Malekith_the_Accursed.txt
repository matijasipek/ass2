Twelve of his older brothers died during a war with the trolls. His mother eventually sold him for two sacks of snake livers and half a barrel of pickled toads. He worked as a body burner, who cremated bodies after a battle, until he was captured by trolls. In his prison he met a wizard, who helped him escape and took him as an apprentice. Eventually the Wizard wanted to combine their powers for peace, but Malekith refused because he felt that any peace would mean that the war which forged him would have been pointless, making his existence something which wasn\'t meant to be. He then killed the wizard, who before dying scarred his face, and then his mother, taking the dogs of the Wild Hunt.<ref>\'\'Thor: God of Thunder\'\' #25. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nLater, Malekith struck an alliance with [[Loki (Marvel Comics)|Loki]] on behalf of the fire demon [[Surtur (Marvel Comics)|Surtur]].<ref>\'\'Thor\'\' #344. Marvel Comics.</ref> He took control of a number of Earth humans using special food of the faerie provided by [[Hela (Marvel Comics)|Hela]]. Malekith then killed Eric Willis, guardian of the [[Casket of Ancient Winters]], after learning its location.<ref>\'\'Thor\'\' #345. Marvel Comics.</ref> As Master of the Hounds, Malekith hunted down Roger Willis, Eric\'s son and new guardian of the Casket. Malekith battled Thor, and kidnapped [[Lorelei (Asgardian)|Lorelei]].<ref>\'\'Thor\'\' #346. Marvel Comics.</ref> Using Lorelei as bait, Malekith forced Thor to battle [[Kurse|Algrim the Strong]], one of his Dark Elf followers, then attempted to destroy both combatants by plunging them into a pool of magma. He then captured the Casket of Ancient Winters from Roger Willis.<ref>\'\'Thor\'\' #347. Marvel Comics.</ref> Malekith was ultimately defeated by Thor, but not before he destroyed the Casket of Ancient Winters, releasing magical frigid force all over the Earth.<ref>\'\'Thor\'\' #348. Marvel Comics.</ref> Malekith was then brought as a captive by Thor to [[Asgard (comics)|Asgard]].<ref>\'\'Thor\'\' #349. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nMalekith later disguised Loki as himself to take his place in the dungeon, while he disguised himself as [[Balder (comics)|Balder]], who was about to be crowned ruler of Asgard. Kurse, the being formerly known as Algrim, saw through Malekith\'s disguise when he reached Asgard City. Kurse then broke Malekith\'s neck, apparently slaying him.<ref>\'\'Thor\'\' #367. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nYears later, Malekith was revealed to be alive once again. Disguising himself as Balder again, he enlisted [[Hercules (Marvel Comics)|Hercules]] to attack Alflyse, the Dark Elf Queen of the Eastern Spires, while disguised as Thor. His scheme unraveled (partially due to the appearance of Thor disguised as Hercules), and he was easily defeated by [[Zeus (Marvel Comics)|Zeus]].<ref>\'\'The Incredible Hercules\'\' #132, 136. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nMalekith has resurfaced again intent on collecting [[Mandarin\'s rings]].<ref>\'\'Iron Man\'\' vol. 5 #22, Kieron Gillen, 2014. Marvel Comics.</ref> The other ring-bearing hosts were later to meet their demise or mutilation at the hands of Malekith. In the storyline "Rings of the Mandarin", it is revealed that after reclaiming his kingdom Svartalfheim he was approached by a ring seeking a host, but bent its will to his rather than letting it control his mind. He has now begun a campaign to attack all other "Mandarins" and take their rings, desiring "the full set" before attacking [[Iron Man|Tony Stark]]. Though he is usually a foe of Thor and other magical beings, his opposition to Iron Man is rooted in Elves\' traditional weakness towards iron.<ref>\'\'Iron Man\'\' vol. 5 #23.NOW. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nMalekith, who escaped from his prison in [[Niffleheim]], rampages across the Nine Realms in a path of revenge, targeting any Dark Elf who isn\'t loyal to him. However, the opposition to Malekith had drawn together the other races of the realms, causing a new level of understanding and cooperation. He possesses superhuman intellect, strength, speed, stamina, durability, agility and reflexes.\n\nAs a dark elf, Malekith also has the ability to manipulate the forces of magic for a variety of effects, including teleportation, energy projection, physical malleability, flight (by transforming into mist), illusion casting, and the ability to change the shape and appearance of other beings or objects.\n\nMalekith, like all Dark Elves, has a vulnerability to iron, which disrupts or cancels his magical spells.\n\n==Other versions==\n\n===Marvel Adventures imprint===\n{{also|Marvel Adventures}}\nMalekith appeared in an issue of \'\'[[Marvel Adventures: The Avengers]]\'\' where he tries to take over [[Asgard (comics)|Asgard]] with the help of the [[Giants (Marvel Comics)|Frost Giants]], but he was defeated with the combined efforts of the Avengers, Thor, and many other Asgardian warriors.<ref>\'\'Marvel Adventures: The Avengers\'\' #15 (October, 2007).
