After his rifle ran out, he ran off with his steel-lined suit protecting him from their bullets. When Rawhide Kid was suspected of robbing a train, Two-Gun Kid advised him to turn himself over. Ace Fenton himself began stirring up the people of Tombstone to turn against the Rawhide Kid claiming that he trained the Grizzly to rob a train. On the day of the trial, Grizzly broke into the courtroom and abducted Rawhide Kid to make it look like they are partners in crime. He tried to kill Rawhide Kid, but this failed and his mask was removed. Rawhide Kid was not able to get a glimpse of the Grizzly\'s face when he ran off. Rawhide Kid and Two-Gun Kid found his empty costume and headed to a saloon that the Grizzly had made on mistake. His violent actions brought him to the attention of [[J. Jonah Jameson]], whose articles got Maxwell expelled from wrestling. Ten years later, he met with the [[Jackal (Marvel Comics)|Jackal]], who gave him a grizzly bear suit and an exoskeleton harness that amplified his strength and durability. He used this harness to attack the [[Daily Bugle]] in an attempt to get revenge against Jameson for ruining his wrestling career, but Spider-Man defeated him.<ref>\'\'The Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #139</ref> The Grizzly then attempted to defeat Spider-Man alongside the Jackal, but was defeated again by Spider-Man and went to jail.<ref>\'\'The Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #140. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nAfter his release, Markham took his grizzly suit and exoskeleton harness to the [[Tinkerer]] for them to be fixed and upgraded. The Grizzly demanded a rematch with Spider-Man so that he could take revenge on Spider-Man and save face among his peers. Spider-Man faked defeat, allowing the Grizzly to think he defeated him.<ref>\'\'Web of Spider-Man\'\' #58. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nHe later joined up with the [[Gibbon (comics)|Gibbon]], the [[Spot (comics)|Spot]], and the [[Kangaroo (comics)|Kangaroo]] II to become the \'\'Legion of Losers\'\'. Planning only to get back at Spider-Man, the Grizzly and the Gibbon were shocked to see the Kangaroo II and the Spot robbing a bank. They did capture Spider-Man, but released him, claiming that "He\'s an all-right guy".<ref>\'\'The Spectacular Spider-Man\'\' vol. 2 #245-246. Marvel Comics.</ref>The Grizzly and the Gibbon teamed up with Spider-Man to capture the Kangaroo II and the Spot. The Grizzly and the Gibbon later became crime-fighters and helped Spider-Man (disguised as the Bombastic Bag-Man) again to stop the [[White Rabbit (comics)|White Rabbit]]\'s bank robbery.<ref>\'\'The Spectacular Spider-Man\'\' vol. 2 #253. Marvel Comics.</ref><ref>\'\'The Spectacular Spider-Man\'\' vol. 2 #256. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nAt one point, out on probation, he tries to turn his life around. His desire to keep wearing the suit, several accidents and the interference of his criminal friends make his life that much more miserable.\n\nHe was brought in by [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] at one point or another, and his interrogation yielded key information that would drive the events of the "[[Secret War (comics)|Secret War]]".\n\nLater, he is receiving legal services from the law offices that employ the She-Hulk.<ref>\'\'[[She-Hulk]]\'\' #6 (May 2006)</ref> He had been accused of a robbery at Madison Square Garden, but his defense was to be that he had been seen fighting [[Power Pack]] in New Jersey at the same time.\n\nThe Grizzly meets [[Starfox (comic character)|Starfox]] moments later.
