Ordered to find a way to defeat [[Thor (Marvel Comics)|Thor]] - who thwarts the Red Army\'s invasion of [[India]] - the character exposes himself to small doses of [[radiation]] until he is able to endure a massive barrage, becoming a living "Radioactive Man". He is defeated by Giant-Man and [[Iron Man]] when they imprison him in [[lead]] coils. He is then deported back to China.<ref>\'\'The Avengers\'\' #6 (July 1964)</ref> The character reappears with the new Masters of Evil, organized by the robot [[Ultron]]. Abilities include radiation emission as heat, "hard" radiation (inflicting opponents with nausea, dizziness, and [[radiation poisoning]]), EMP emissions to deactivate machinery, [[hypnotic]] light and so much more.<ref>\'\'Iron Man Legacy\'\' #5 (Aug 2010)</ref><ref>\'\'Journey into Mystery\'\' #93 (June 1963).</ref> He can also absorb nuclear radiation without harm and convert it to energy for personal use such as physical enhancement or to [[cauterize]] wounds,<ref>\'\'New Thunderbolts\'\' #3-4 (2005)</ref><ref>\'\'Thunderbolts\'\' #115 (Aug. 2007)</ref> create energy constructs such as [[force shield|force fields]] or golems out of radiation wit greater physical strength & resilience than his own,<ref>\'\'Deadpool & the Mercs for Money\'\' vol. 2 #2 (Aug. 2016)</ref> convert different energies into any other radiology form along the electromagnetic spectrum.<ref>\'\'Thor: First Thunder\'\' #4 (Dec. 2010)</ref> He has shown the ability to leave behind "radiation pills", i.e. infusing a single individual with enough radiation to give him severe [[radiation poisoning]] and let the contamination spread in a large population.<ref>\'\'New Thunderbolts\'\' #9 (Aug. 2005)</ref> While it was briefly implied that his radioactive makeup along with some further enhancements of his powers had left him radioactive and unable to have contact with unshielded people, he was [[retcon]]ned to have always been in full control of his abilities. Even so, during his tenure in the Thunderbolts, Norman Osborn had his original costume replaced with a customized radiation suit, hiding his Asian features and quelling every lingering fear about his radioactive makeup. Chen Lu holds a Ph.D. in nuclear physics.\n\nIgor Stancheck, Kid and Warhead have the same or at least similar powers.\n\n==Other versions==\n\n===Heroes Reborn===\nIn the [[Heroes Reborn (1996 comic)|Heroes Reborn]] universe, created by [[Franklin Richards (Fantastic Four)|Franklin Richards]], Radioactive Man appeared as a member of [[Loki (Marvel Comics)|Loki]]\'s [[Masters of Evil]].<ref>\'\'Avengers\'\' vol. 2 #8</ref>\n\n===MC2===\nRadioactive Man is still active in the [[Marvel Comics 2|MC2]] reality.{{issue|date=April 2013}}\n\n===House of M===\nThe Radioactive Man makes an appearance in the \'\'[[House of M]]\'\' reality, on the team of villains  who attempted to break [[Baron von Strucker]] out of prison.
