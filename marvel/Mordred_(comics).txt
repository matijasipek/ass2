He was one of the Knights of Camelot in Britain of the 6th Century, A.D. [[Merlin (Marvel Comics)#Merlin of Legend|Merlin]] warned Arthur that Mordred would be responsible for the end of Camelot, but before Arthur could put the baby to death, he was rescued and raised in anonymity. Mordred was an insurrectionist, and his many conspiracies against his father were foiled by his constant foe Sir Percy of Scandia (the original [[Black Knight (Sir Percy)|Black Knight]]) and others until finally Mordred\'s forces defeated Arthur\'s on [[Salisbury Plain]].{{issue|date=February 2013}} Arthur and Mordred fatally wounded each other, and before dying, Mordred mortally wounded the Black Knight as well. Mordred was taken to Castle Scandia, where he died.{{issue|date=February 2013}}\n\nIn modern times, Mordred\'s spirit has been allowed by the evil Celtic "Nether Gods" he serves to become active on Earth. His first opponent was fittingly the new [[Black Knight (Dane Whitman)|Black Knight]], Dane Whitman. He transformed former knife-thrower Paul Richarde into Le Sabre and sent him against the modern Black Knight, but was defeated.<ref>\'\'Marvel Super-Heroes\'\' #17</ref> Mordred took human form in 12th Century Europe, where he formed an alliance with Prince John against King Richard the Lion-Hearted. Mordred was opposed by the reincarnated Black Knight. Mordred encountered the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]], and was defeated by the time-traveling [[Prester John (comics)|Prester John]].<ref>\'\'The Defenders\'\' #11</ref> Mordred again was commanded by the Nethergods Mandrac and Necromon to prevent the arrival of Black Knight and Captain Britain to Otherworld. Mordred was defeated by Merlyn when the heroes finally arrived in Otherworld. Angry with Mordred\'s failure, Necromon sent him to his deepest dungeon.<ref>\'\'Hulk Comic UK\'\' (1979) #1-63</ref>\n\nMordred\'s spirit was drawn into the netherworld by [[Morgan le Fay (Marvel Comics)|Morgan le Fay]], and they became lovers. He plotted with her to change the Earth into a realm of black magic. With Morgan, he sent the [[Dreadknight]] against the Black Knight, [[Doctor Strange]], and Victoria Bentley.<ref>\'\'Black Knight\'\' vol. 2 #2</ref> With Morgan le Fay, he sent Balor of the Fomor against the Black Knight, Doctor Strange, and [[Valkyrie (Marvel Comics)|Valkyrie]] (Victoria Bentley).<ref>\'\'Black Knight\'\' vol. 2 #3</ref> With Morgan le Fay, he send the Fomor and "Wicker Man" against the Black Knight, Doctor Strange, and Valkyrie. Mordred was defeated and returned to the netherworld by the Black Knight.<ref>\'\'Black Knight\'\' vol. 2 #4</ref> Mordred later assisted Morgan in a scheme involving the Twilight Sword, which reunited the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]].{{issue|date=February 2013}}\n\nAfter menacing the Black Knights, Mordred remained dead for some time. During this time, his father King Arthur became a pawn of Merlyn, and a sworn enemy of mutantkind, which he and his allies called "Witchbreed" or "Friends of Mordred". Through this [[Excalibur (comics)|Excalibur]] were able to figure out that Mordred was a mutant.<ref>\'\'Excalibur\'\' Vol 4 23 (September 2021)</ref>\n\nOnce King Arthur confirmed this and that Mordred was prophesied to overthrow him, they returned to Krakoa and convinced the Five to resurrect him, using a vial of Otherworldly water in place of the standard genetic material. The Otherworldly magic involved made for an atypical resurrection, as Mordred immediately disappeared from the egg and found himself in [[Avalon (Marvel Comics)|Otherworld]], returned to a young age and unaware of how he got there. Arthur\'s forces quickly engaged him in combat, but he proved a worthy foe. He eventually found his way to [[Betsy Braddock|Captain Britain]] and her companions, he presented himself and asked for asylum. After the factory was destroyed by [[Rictor (comics)|Rictor]] and [[Shatterstar]] with Gia\'s help, the team headed toward Savalith, an ancient civilization founded by vampire-like beings.<ref>\'\'Knights of X\'\' #2 (June 2022)</ref> After regrouping to save [[Mad Jim Jaspers]] in The Crooked Marked, [[Gambit (comics)|Gambit]] sacrificed himself against King Arthur\'s forces gaining the team access to Mercator, an Otherworld domain created by [[Mister M (comics)|Mister M]] out of the Siege Perilous. There they sank into the quicksand and were psychologically tested by the land, until [[Rachel Summers]] was able to break them out of the illusion.
