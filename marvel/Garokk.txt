Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nZaladane later captured an adventurer called \'\'\'Kirk Marston\'\'\' and magically resurrected Garokk in the body of Kirk Marston using the ashes of the deceased previous Petrified Man. Marston\'s body quickly transformed into a duplicate of Garokk\'s own. With Zaladane, Garokk attempted to unite the Savage Land tribes by forcing them to erect a colossal city for them. Garokk and Zaladane fought the [[X-Men]] and Ka-Zar; Garokk was defeated by [[Cyclops (Marvel Comics)|Cyclops]] and fell down into the city\'s thermal shaft.<ref>\'\'The Uncanny X-Men\'\' #115-116. Marvel Comics.</ref> The X-Men believed Garokk to be dead. The X-Men\'s enemy [[Magneto (Marvel Comics)|Magneto]] later found Garokk, who had transformed: he was larger and half his body had turned into crystal while the other half became like molten rock, and his intelligence was dulled. Magneto forced Garokk to serve as the guardian of his Antarctic base. In this role, he fought the X-Man [[Storm (Marvel Comics)|Storm]], but again fell in a deep pit.<ref>\'\'The Uncanny X-Men\'\' #149. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nAgain Garokk survived and he returned to his normal size and appearance. When the Savage Land was destroyed by the alien [[Terminus (comics)|Terminus]], Terminus was defeated by the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]], his armor ruptured and Terminus himself left to die, but it turned out that this Terminus was just an impostor. Garokk found a suit of Terminus armor and under mental control (presumably by the real Terminus) entered it and started destroying the remainder of the Savage Land. The X-Men fought this "Terminus" and destroyed the armor to reveal Garokk. Garokk regained his free will and together with a machine made by the [[High Evolutionary]], he restored the tropical environment to the Savage Land. This process infused Garokk\'s essence into the land, causing him to cease to exist as an individual entity.<ref>\'\'The Uncanny X-Men\'\' Annual #12. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nGarokk returned years later as an enemy of Ka-Zar, but Ka-Zar defeated him.<ref>\'\'Ka-Zar\'\' Annual \'97</ref><ref>\'\'Ka-Zar\'\' vol. 2 #20. Marvel Comics.</ref> He then led the Avengers to the High Evolutionary\'s hidden base.<ref>\'\'Avengers\'\' vol. 5 #13 (Aug. 2013). Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nA human is transformed into a manifestation of Garokk by the mutagenic effects of liquid from the fiery underground pool in the Savage Land. Garokk\'s skin and much, perhaps all, of his body tissue are transformed into organic, stone-like substances, giving him gray skin that seems rock-like and "petrified" in appearance. He has superhuman stamina and his petrified body also makes him difficult to injure. Garokk has the ability to project tremendous amounts of heat, light, and concussive force from his eyes. He can tap into other energy sources to replenish his own powers. Garokk has the ability to create dimensional warps with the energy projected from his eyes, and is capable of transporting an entire city through one of these dimensional portals. He has the ability to change his size and transform into a being of pure energy, and then back into his stone-like physical form at will{{Citation needed|date=June 2010}}. Garokk can also manipulate matter on a sub-atomic scale; he could rearrange the very fabric of the Savage Land. He has a limited telepathic ability which allows him to learn of the activities of his worshippers through his dreams and his mental connection to his followers.\n\nGarokk is immortal; he does not age and even when his body is destroyed, he can be returned to life. Immersion in the pool which gave him his powers will cause Garokk to revert to human form.
