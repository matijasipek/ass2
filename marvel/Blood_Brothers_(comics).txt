When [[Iron Man]] receives a mental signal sent by an imprisoned [[Drax the Destroyer]], he becomes a target for the Blood Brothers, who ambush him and take him to Thanos\' base where Drax is being held. When the two heroes overpower the brothers, Thanos detonates an explosion, forcing the heroes to flee, leaving the Blood Brothers behind.<ref>\'\'Iron Man\'\' #55 (Feb. 1973)</ref> When Thanos later obtains the [[Cosmic Cube]], Iron Man returns to Thanos\' base to search for him, and is taken by surprise by the Blood Brothers, who are still alive, and remain loyal to Thanos. They attempt to kill Iron Man, but he and the [[Thing (comics)|Thing]], who saw Iron Man\'s flight overhead and followed him to the base, overpower the Blood Brothers. With the henchmen defeated a second time, Thanos teleports the Blood Brothers to an unrevealed location.<ref>\'\'Marvel Feature\'\' #12 (Nov. 1973)</ref>\n\nThey reappear years later in the service of the villain the [[Controller (Marvel Comics)|Controller]], and battle Iron Man once more. Iron Man defeats them both with the aid of the hero [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]].<ref>\'\'Iron Man\'\' #88-89 (Aug.-Sept. 1976)</ref> Sometime later, the United States military discovers Thanos\' old Earth base in [[Arizona]] and, after activating some of the machinery, accidentally teleports in the Blood Brothers. The two battle the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] and are eventually depowered by Thanos\' brother, [[Starfox (comics)|Eros]].<ref>\'\'The Avengers\'\' #252 (Jan. 1990)</ref>\n\nThe Blood Brothers are imprisoned, but are later released by the hero [[Quasar (comics)|Quasar]] and left on the planet Mars.<ref>\'\'Quasar\'\' #12 (July 1990)</ref> They later attempt to stop [[Rogue (comics)|Rogue]] of the [[X-Men]] from rescuing a [[Mutant (Marvel Comics)|mutant]] extraterrestrial of the [[Skrull]] race.<ref>\'\'X-Men\'\' #107 (Dec. 2000)</ref> The brothers later fight alongside Earth\'s metahumans against an invasion by [[Ego the Living Planet]], but are later imprisoned with alien criminals [[Paibok|Paibok the Power Skrull]] and [[Lunatik]].<ref>\'\'Maximum Security\'\' (Dec. 2000-Jan. 2001)</ref> The pair eventually escape and crash-land on Earth. They help enslave a small Alaskan town in an attempt to craft a space-worthy ship. This fails, due to the intervention of Drax the Destroyer, where one brother is apparently killed.<ref>\'\'Drax the Destroyer\'\' #1-4 (Nov. 2005-Feb. 2006)</ref> The second brother, however survives, and is later seen escaping the Raft, a floating prison for supervillains.<ref>\'\'The New Avengers\'\' #1 (Nov. 2004)</ref>\n\nThe surviving Blood Brother later joins the [[Hood (comics)|Hood]] and his criminal empire,<ref>\'\'The New Avengers\'\' #35 (Oct. 2007)</ref> and helps fight the alien shape-shifting race the [[Skrulls]] during the [[Secret Invasion|Skrull invasion of Earth]].<ref>\'\'Secret Invasion\'\' #6 (Sept. 2008)</ref> He joins with the Hood\'s gang in an attack on the New Avengers, who were expecting the [[Dark Avengers]] instead.<ref>\'\'The New Avengers\'\' #50</ref> When [[Jonas Harrow]], one of the many members of Hood\'s syndicate, develops a power drainer, the surviving Brother is part of the splinter group that forms and rebels against Norman Osborn\'s power structure. Most of this confrontation takes place in New York City\'s Times Square.<ref>\'\'The New Avengers\'\' #56-57 (Oct-Nov 2009)</ref> This ends when Harrow is killed by the Hood.{{issue|date=January 2014}}\n\nOnce again under command of Norman Osborn, the Blood Brother is part of the many villainous forces sent in as part of [[Siege (comics)|Siege]] of [[Asgard (comics)|Asgard]], a plan which ends with the sibling\'s severe wounding.<ref>\'\'The New Avengers\'\' #63</ref>\n\nIn the crossover series \'\'[[The Black Vortex]]\'\', the Blood Brother is shown to be wasting away without his brother\'s life force to sustain him. [[J\'son (comics)|Mister Knife]] exposes him to the titular artefact, which transforms him into a new, more powerful form (which Knife christens \'\'\'Brother Blood\'\'\'), and inducts him into his \'\'\'Slaughter Lords,\'\'\' alongside other beings who have been exposed to the Vortex\'s energies.<ref>\'\'Guardians of the Galaxy & X-Men: The Black Vortex Alpha\'\' #1 (Feb. 2015)</ref>\n\nDuring the \'\'[[Avengers: Standoff!]]\'\' storyline, the Blood Brothers were inmates of Pleasant Hill, a gated community established by S.H.I.E.L.D. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nEach Blood Brother\'s tremendous strength and durability is dependent on his proximity to his twin. When standing together, the two have a high degree of superhuman strength, but if separated weaken to below even normal human levels.
